    Protected Space

    What is an optimal protected space/how to choose a protected space?

    An optimal protected space is the best protected space you can reach in a few minutes upon receiving a preliminary guideline, and enter in accordance with the time you have available to reach shelter, based to the following:

    • In the private space – residential protected room (Mamad), shared protected room (Mamak) or shared shelter.
    • In the public space: public shelters, standard protected spaces in educational institutions and municipal facilities, and temporary public shelters approved by the Home Front Command. Before heading to public protected spaces - you must check with the local authority where they are located and if they have been opened. Remember: Do not choose parking lots that have not been approved by the Home Front Command as a protected space, it’s life-threatening.
    • Only if there is no public protected space near you, choose an inner stairwell, without windows and external walls.
    • Only if there is no other option - choose an inner space in the house, surrounded by as many walls as possible and with as few windows and openings as possible.
    How can I know where there is a public shelter or a in my residential area / if the nearby shelter or temporary public shelter temporary public shelter / or if the nearby shelter or temporary public shelter is closed, who is responsible for opening it?

    Check with the local authority where the relevant public protected spaces are located and make sure they are opened before you go there. Make sure in advance that they can be reached within a few minutes of receiving a guideline.

    Can an underground parking lot be used as a protected space?

    You can enter only temporary public shelters that were approved by the Home Front Command. Do not choose parking lots that have not been approved by the Home Front Command as a protected space, this is life-threatening.

    Why is using a parking lot that was not approved by the Home Front Command as a protected space life-threatening?

    A parking lot that was not approved to serve as a temporary public shelter by the Home Front Command engineers may become a trap for those staying in them, because the parking lot ceiling might collapse in case of a missile hit. Therefore, only choose a protected space approved by Home Front Command experts.

    Are the stairwell / inner room not good enough to serve as a protected space?

    The optimal protection is a residential protected room (Mamad), shared protected room (Mamak), shared shelter or public protected space. Only if you cannot reach these protected spaces within a few minutes of receiving the preliminary guideline - choose an inner stairwell without windows and external walls. Only if there is no other option - choose an inner space in the house, surrounded by as many walls as possible and with as few windows and openings as possible. The stairwell provides a protective response because it is surrounded by apartments and constitutes as the buildings’ foundation and is therefore stronger than other parts of the structure. The inner space is more protected compared to other areas in the house because it is surrounded by walls.

    How should I act if my optimal protected space is the stairwell?

    A stairwell should be chosen as a protected space only if there is no protected room (Mamad), shared protected room (Mamak), shared shelter or public protected space that you can reach within a few minutes of receiving the preliminary guideline, or in the time you have available to reach shelter. The stairwell must be without windows and external walls in order to serve as a protected space. You should stay on the stairs and not in the floor space. In a building over three stories—stay on a stairwell that has at least two stories above it. In a building under three stories—stay on the stairwell of the middle floor. Do not stay on the entrance floor and the top floor.

    How should I act if my optimal protected space is an inner space?

    Choose an inner space in the house as a protected space only if there is no residential protected room (Mamad), shared protected room (Mamak), shared shelter, public protected space or inner stairwell that can be reached within a few minutes of receiving the preliminary guideline or in the time you have available to reach shelter when receiving an alert. The inner space must be surrounded by as many walls as possible and with as few windows and openings as possible, without ceramic, porcelain or glass that could shatter. An internal corridor can also be chosen, closing the doors and windows. Upon receiving an alert—sit close to an inner wall, below the window line and not in front of the door.

    Can the bathroom/toilet/kitchen be chosen as a protected space?

    Do not choose a bathroom or toilet as a protected space, and make sure there is no ceramic, porcelain or glass in the protected space that could shatter.

    I have difficulty moving. What are the key points for choosing and preparing a protected space?

    You must choose and prepare the protected space in advance, including the path leading to it, to ensure maximum accessibility and convenience for reaching it within a few minutes of receiving a preliminary guideline, or within the time you have available to reach shelter when receiving an alert. Remove all objects that may interfere with movement and secure essential equipment and items so they do not fall. It is important to practice reaching the protected space in advance and to clear the path of obstacles to prevent falls and trips along the way.

     

    In your chosen protected space, make sure that windows are closed throughout the day to reduce entry and organization time, and verify that it contains an emergency kit with all the essential equipment you need.

     

    Additionally, prepare a bag packed in a way that can be attached to a wheelchair or walker containing your personal equipment. Remember to include a document indicating your medical condition, a list of phone numbers for people and organizations that assist you regularly, medications you take on a regular basis, and specific spare equipment such as a walking stick, oxygen equipment, etc.

    I have difficulty moving and it is not possible for me to reach a public protected space within a few minutes. What should I do?

    If it is not possible to reach a public protected space within a few minutes, choose an inner stairwell or an inner space surrounded by as many walls as possible and with as few windows and openings as possible. It is recommended to place a chair in the protected space in advance.

    How should I choose a protected space if there is a mobility difficulty or if I am bedridden?
    • If there is a difficulty in moving from a sofa to a wheelchair in a short time—position the sofa against an inner wall, below the window line and not in front of the door.
    • If you use a wheelchair or a walker, or are bedridden—move the bed to the residential protected room (Mamad) or an inner space and position it against an inner wall, below the window line and not in front of the door. Make sure there are no shelves or objects that could fall installed above the bed.
