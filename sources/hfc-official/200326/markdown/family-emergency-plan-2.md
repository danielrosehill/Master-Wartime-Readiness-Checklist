## In routine and emergency situations, it is important to gather the members of the household and plan a family emergency plan, which will include a family conversation, division of roles, preparation of the protected space, and practice.

Preparing a family emergency plan makes it easier to make decisions during an emergency and increases the sense of efficiency and control over the situation. The plan can be prepared by all family members, giving each one a role and making the children play an active and important part in preparing for an emergency.

### You can divide the preparation of the family plan to three stages:

**1. Talking:** Hold a family conversation during which we will plan and exercise the actions we need to perform facing an emergency. This conversion includes, familiarizing with the various emergency situations, the method of which we should prepare for them, and how we should deal with them.

**2. Delegating:** Divide the work between all family members in preparing the home and [the equipment for emergencies](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/articles/info/preparing-protected-space/1201/ "ציוד לשעת חירום"), including organizing the supplies for the emergency bag and storing it (best to put it inside the Residential Protected Room).  
In addition, tasks to be performed during an emergency should be assigned, for example, ensuring that all family members are in the protected room. It is important to divide the roles according to age and abilities, and to take into account unique characteristics, for example, if there is a family member who requires assistance with walking, or a pet that must be taken with us. It is important to give roles to everyone, as the mere participation and taking responsibility contribute to improving the sense of security and the ability to cope.

It is important to divide the roles according to age and abilities. It should also be made clear that the role of each family member does not replace and contradict the actions that everyone should perform during an emergency, and think about a different division of roles in the event one or more family member is absent.

**3. Drills :**Possible emergencies and threats change from time to time, as does the composition of the household and the age of the family members. Therefore, periodic family meetings should be held to practice how to deal with each scenario, for example, entering the protected room according to the time available to you.

During those drill, make sure you check:  

**Assignments of roles amongst family members** - If new assignment were added.  
 **Update supplies** - such as water bottles or the emergency lighting batteries.

[Means of Communications](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/articles/emergency-preparedness/preparing-routine/2105/ "למי פונים בזמן חירום?") - each family member should know who to contact in case of an emergency.  

**Follow-up** - ensure that every family member is making progress on their assignments, such as fixing objects in place or replacing supplies.

### **Important point for the family meeting**

An important part of a family’s preparation for emergency situations is holding a family discussion, in which family members are provided with knowledge about the various threats, how to cope with them and strengthen the family's sense of competence so that it becomes the central framework for coping and support. **Steps for conducting a family discussion:**

**1. What is an emergency?**  

Define together what is an emergency situation and give the children an opportunity to provide examples from their own world. List the various kinds of emergencies, explain the dangers in each one and provide tools to identify the different emergencies as they occur, for example:

• A rising and falling siren = a missile attack.  
• Moving furniture and the feeling of ‘being on a boat’ = an earthquake.  
• The smell of smoke and sudden heat = fire in one of the areas of the house.

**2. How do you prepare?** Analyze the preparation process in each emergency. For example:

• A missile attack: identify and prepare the protected space in your home ([how to choose a protected space](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/articles/info/preparing-protected-space/1200/ "הנחיות לבחירת המרחב המוגן")), prepare the supplies and how they should be used ([which supplies you need](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/articles/info/preparing-protected-space/1201/ "ציוד לשעת חירום")), [know the alert system](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/articles/info/iron-swords/1109), your area of alert and the time available you have to reach a protected space ([for localized alerts](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/articles/protection-guidelines/preparing-protected-space/1103/ "מה זמן הכניסה למרחב המוגן?")) and the [guidelines of what to do if an alert is received.](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/life-saving-guidelines/rocket-and-missile-attacks)  
• An earthquake: fix heavy objects in place and clear the evacuation route from obstacles and objects that pose a risk (such as items that can block the path or flammable items), knowledge of warning signs and signs indicating an earthquake, and knowledge of [guidelines for behavior during an earthquake.](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/life-saving-guidelines/earthquake)

### **Pay attention to the following points:**

**• Knowing the gas and electricity points** - all family members should know where the gas and electricity points are located in the house, under what circumstances the taps should be turned off and how.  

**• Locating weak points** - It is necessary to specify places that are not recommended to be in an emergency, for example inside bathrooms and bathtubs due to the fear of breaking porcelain, near heavy objects, near windows and glass objects and inside elevators.  

**• Protected places outside the home** - We will make sure that the children locate safe places in and around school, near playgrounds and anywhere they often spend time, and we will explain the situation to them in a way that will allow them to protect themselves even in unfamiliar places.  

**• Location of family emergency equipment items** - We will show family members where the emergency equipment items are stored (preferably stored in a protected room), we will note items that we did not include in the emergency bag (such as emergency lighting), and we will make sure that family members are familiar with each item and know how and when to use it.

**3. Order of actions** - Plan with family members the automatic order of actions to be performed according to each scenario.   
The following is an automatic order of actions that you can memorize:

* Notify the relevant rescue entity or an adult as early as possible.
* Close the gas and electricity taps to prevent the spread and ignition of fire.
* Distance - moving to a protected space based on the current event, for example, entering the protected space during rocket and missile attack.
* Closure and isolation - adding barriers between us and the threat, for example, closing windows and doors in the protected space during rocket and missile attack.
* Action - for example, moving to a [safe place](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/articles/earthquake/preparation-coping/2504) during an earthquake.
* Attention - listening to the instructions of the security forces in the field or through the media.

**4. Meeting place** - We will set up meeting places around the house and in a more remote place such as a building or public park. We will make sure that family members know the place, know how to identify it and get there, and remind them to take the emergency bag in case of immediate evacuation, if it is possible to do so.

**5. Who should be notified?** Make sure that all family members are familiar with the [phone list](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/articles/info/preparing-protected-space/1211) for emergencies that was prepared as part of preparing the house, and that they know the location of the list. In this context, it is required to go over the details that are important to provide when making an emergency call - a phone number to return to in the event of a call being disconnected, what is happening in the house, residential address, location in the house (especially in the event of a fire or collapse of the building), who else is in the house and what their condition is.

### **Emphases for conducting the family conversation**

**Planning in advance** - we will plan in advance the order of things and the important points to note.  

**Full family presence** - the conversation should be held in the presence of all family members. If there are age gaps, the conversation can be split between the older children and the younger children, to allow everyone to express themselves according to their needs and abilities.  

**Involving all family members** - It is the parents' responsibility to ensure that each family member can express themselves and that an atmosphere of openness is created. It is also important not to disadvantage anyone in the distribution of roles, as the mere participation and taking responsibility contribute to improving the sense of security and the ability to cope.  

**Empowerment** - We will try to emphasize the importance of each person's area of ​​responsibility and the importance of their contribution to the success of the family preparation.  

**When to hold the family conversation** - In a family with adult children, it is better to hold the conversation at the beginning of the preparation process and share with them the considerations in dividing tasks, choosing the protected space, preparing the house and collecting and storing equipment. In a family with young children, it is better for the conversation to summarize the preparation and serve mainly to present the protected space, equipment, etc.  

**Attention to body language and choice of words** - Verbal and non-verbal messages affect the way our children interpret the situation. During the conversation, it is recommended to give short and truthful answers that convey messages of confidence, coping, and cohesion ("We are together"), using words and concepts from the content worlds that are understandable to children. It is also important to explain that we are raising the issue to ensure their safety in possible situations and not as preparation for a certain event.  

**Explaining the logic behind the considerations** - It is important to explain, even to small children, that the meaning of advance preparation is to improve the readiness of family members in a way that will allow us to preserve our lives in case of need. This way, even if we cannot act in real time according to the course of action we determined in the family conversation, our children will be able to find appropriate alternatives.  

**Legitimizing concerns and fears** - Giving family members the opportunity to reveal their anxieties and concerns will allow them to understand that none of us are alone in our feelings.  

**Summary** - At the end of the conversation, we will make a general summary of the main points that came up in the conversation and schedule periodic meetings to practice preparation. In this framework, we will direct the participants' attention to the preparedness and not to the emergency situation and ensure that the preparedness contributed to the feeling of competence.

What is the right equipment to prepare for an emergency?

It is important to prepare in advance the necessary equipment that will assist us and our household members during an emergency, and to store it inside a bag that we can take with us when needed.  
For the emergency equipment list, [click here](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/articles/info/preparing-protected-space/1201/ "נערכים למצבי חירום עם כל הציוד הנדרש - איראן 7").

What emergencies require to prepare equipment ahead of time?

In emergency situations that require the population to remain in lockdown for an extended period of time, in situations where it is necessary to evacuate the house, and in situations in whichf damage to infrastructure may damage the fabric of life. A mass casualty incident also requires preparing equipment in advance because a large number of cases will force the rescue forces to set priorities for handling cases and will extend the time of arrival to each home and each patient.

What are the phone numbers of call centers that provide emotional support and help during emergencies?

[For a list of phone number for call centers that provide emotional support and help](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/articles/info/emergency-management/1307/ "חשים מתח? עצות להתמודדות").

Why is it important to prepare emergency phone numbers in advance?

Past experience shows that in emergency and distress situations, it is important to involve the emergency entities, the local municipality and other factors such as family members, acquaintances and neighbors, who can help as soon as possible. This is due to the following reasons:  

• Time in an emergency event is critical - in emergency and distress situations, it is important to involve relevant people and entities as soon as possible. A quick arrival of the emergency forces will help in a life-saving response. For example, a quick arrival of the fire and rescue forces will allow to rescue trapped people and put out the fire faster, and a quick arrival of medical forces will make it possible to provide first aid quickly, which can save lives.  
• Remote assistance - the emergency factors can provide essential guidance from a distance, for example in providing assistance to the injured until the arrival of the ambulance and paramedics. However, it is always better to be prepared to deal independently with possible scenarios.  
• The development of emergency events is unknown - during an emergency, there may be situations in which we will not be able to contact the emergency and rescue forces within a short time. That is why it is important to prepare in advance a phone list of family members, acquaintances and neighbors who can help in an emergency.

Why should water be prepared in emergency equipment?

There are emergency situations in which the regular water supply may be affected, so it is important to prepare water in the amount of three liters per person per day, for three days.
