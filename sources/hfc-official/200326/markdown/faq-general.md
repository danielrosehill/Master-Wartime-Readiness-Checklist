## Guidelines During a Rocket and Missile Attack

How to act when receiving an alert for a rocket and missile attack?

When receiving an alert, enter the protected space within the time available to reach shelter, and stay there for 10 minutes or until you receive a message the event has ended.  

[For all behavioral guidelines when receiving an alert](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/life-saving-guidelines/rocket-and-missile-attacks/ "ירי רקטות וטילים- מעודכן").

How should you act when you enter the residential protected room (Mamad) or shared protected room (Mamak) or shelter?

Close the door tightly by turning the handle upwards at a 90 degrees angle, and make sure the external steel window and the inner glass window are closed until the end of the operation to prevent entry of blast waves. If a double wing glass window is installed - remove the wings ahead of time and place it outside the room.

How long until we can leave the protected space?

it is required to stay in the protected space for 10 minutes or until you receive a message the event has ended.

May we leave the protected space after hearing an explosion?

No. Remain in the protected area for 10 minutes or until receiving notice that the event has ended.

How to act when receiving an alert while traveling in a vehicle?

Stop at the side of the road, get out of the vehicle and enter a shelter or a stairwell in a nearby building. If you cannot reach a building within the time available to reach shelter - get out and move away from the vehicle to the side of the road or beyond the guard rail (if there is one), lie on the ground and protect your head with your hands.   
Only if you cannot exit the vehicle, stop at the side of the road, turn the engine off, open the windows and bend below the window line

If an alert is received while driving a vehicle and I am under a bridge - should I get out of the vehicle?

Staying inside a vehicle under a bridge does not protect us from a nearby impact. You need to stop at the side of the road, exit the vehicle and enter a nearby building. If you cannot reach a building within the time available to reach shelter - get out and move away from the vehicle beyond the side of the road or the guard rail, lie on the ground and protect your head with your hands.

Only if it is impossible to exit the vehicle - stop on the side of the road, open the windows, and crouch below the level of the windows.

What does one do when one is in a tunnel during a missile alert?

The vehicle should be stopped with care in the safety bay and wait inside the vehicle for 10 minutes.

How to act when receiving an alert when traveling on public transportation?

**On intercity lines and student buses** - the driver must stop at the side of the road and open the doors, and the passengers must bend below the bus's window line and protect their head with their hands.

**On intra-city bus lines** - the driver must stop at the side of the road and open the doors so that passengers can get off the bus and enter a nearby building. If reaching a building is not possible within the time available, bend below the bus's window line and protect your head with your hands.

**On trains** - the driver must slow down to 30 km/h for 10 minutes and the passengers must bend below the window line in the carriages and protect their head with their hands.

How to act when receiving an alert when travelling by light railway?

**In an overground line** - drivers must stop and open the doors and passengers must bend under the line of the windows and protect their heads with their hands for 10 minutes.

**In an underground line** - the train will keep traveling with no guidelines for passengers.If the train is close to the exit from the tunnel near the area where the alert is received, the train will stop in the underground tunnel and passengers must remain in their seats for 10 minutes.

What are the guidelines for student transportation?

If an alert is received while the bus is in transit, the driver will stop the bus, and the children should crouch below the window line and protect their heads with their hands. Students with physical disabilities who use wheelchairs should remain in their seats inside the bus. The bus can resume driving after 10 minutes.

How to act when receiving an alert while staying outdoors?

One should enter a shelter or stairwell in a nearby building. If this is not possible, one should lie flat on the ground and cover one's head with one's hands for 10 minutes.

How to act when receiving an alert if our protected space is a stairwell?

Sit on the stairwell and not in the floor space. In a building with more than three floors - go to a stairwell that has at least two floors above it. In a building with less than three floors - stay in the stairwell of the middle floor. Do not stay in the bottom floor.

Is it permissible to use an elevator during a missile alert?

Do not use the elevator during a rocket and missile alert. Using the elevator endangers life, as it may be damaged by the blast and cause passengers to be trapped inside.

In the event that the protected space is on a different floor (such as a floor-level shelter or a communal shelter), it must be ensured that it can be reached within the time available to reach shelter, using the stairs only.

If the protected space is a stairwell in which elevators are installed, one should keep away from the elevator door and not stay near it, for fear of impact from the elevator doors.

In addition, if the effect of the blast is felt on the structure – the elevator is not to be used until it has been inspected and certified for use by a qualified technician.

How to act when receiving an alert while inside a prefabricated structure?

If an alert is received while your are inside a building that does not include a concrete ceiling and four walls made of concrete or blocks, such as a prefabricated structure, a prefabricated building, or a plaster or wooden structure, leave the building and enter a protected space within the time available to seek shelter. If it is not possible to enter a protected space within the available time, exit the building, lie on the ground and protect your head with your hands.

What should be done after entering a protected room upon receiving of an alert?

Upon receiving an alert, one should enter a protected room, close the door and ensure that it is locked by pulling the handle up at a 90 degree angle. In addition, at the onset of an emergency situation, the steel window and the inner window should be closed until the emergency situation is over, if a double-winged glass window is installed, it should be removed and taken out of the protected room.

What should I do if an alert is received during a mass event?

If an alert is received during a mass gathering, it is the responsibility of the organizers to stop the event immediately upon receiving the alert and inform the audience. All participants in the event should lie flat on the ground and protect their heads with their hands. If an alert is received during a sit-down event and it is not possible to lie down on the ground - one should stoop as low as possible to lower the silhouette of the body and protect the head with the hands. Note - do not run to the protected room as the running of all participants to the shelter may create a crush and overload on the exit ways, which may lead to trampling of people and injuries.

Why is it important to avoid running to the shelter area upon receiving an alert?

Running to the shelter endangers us with falls and bruises. Therefore, upon receiving an alert, one should proceed to the shelter quickly, but with caution. It is important to check the [time available to reach shelter](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/articles/info/iron-swords/1103/), to choose the protected space accordingly, to plan the route to it and to clear it of obstacles. The time at our disposal allows us to take a deep breath and reach the shelter in a calm and secure manner.

What should be done if unexploded ordnance and unidentified objects are detected?

In the area where a rocket or interception fragment fell, there is a concern of unexploded ordnance remaining on the ground and therefore extreme caution is advised. If you see unexploded ordnance or unidentified objects - stay away from the area, keep others away and report to the Israel Police at [100](tel:100).

[For full guidelines in the vicinity of duds.](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/articles/info/iron-swords/1102/)

Why is it important to lie on the ground when receiving an alert while outdoors ?

In most of the area exposed to rocket and missile impact, the trajectory of the shrapnel is diagonal and upwards. This is why it is better to be as close to the ground as possible in order to minimize the body's silhouette and exposure.

The ground itself can also provide protection, and any protrusions in it - such as mounds, curbs, low railings or ditches - greatly improve the level of protection.

Percentage-wise, in an open area, the probability of being hit by the blast of a missile landing nearby is 100% when standing up and 85% when kneeling - compared with only 10% when lying down. Therefore, although lying down involves discomfort and the concern of staining our clothes, following these guidelines saves lives.

Don't the IDF's defense systems provide hermetic protection?

The IDF's defense systems do not guarantee hermetic defense. Therefore, it is important to adhere to the instructions of the Home Front Command and to use the extra time at our disposal to better prepare ourselves.

How should one act if a preliminary guideline or an alert is received during student transportation?

If a preliminary guideline is received during the journey and there are several minutes to reach a nearby standard protected space, the driver will prepare to stop in a safe location and the students must exit the bus and reach the standard protected space.  
If a standard protected space cannot be reached, the driver will carefully stop in a safe place at the side of the road and open the transportation vehicle's doors, and upon receiving the alert (siren), the students will remain in their places and bend below the window line.  
In the Central Command (Pakmaz) only - the driver will continue driving until reaching a safe place.
