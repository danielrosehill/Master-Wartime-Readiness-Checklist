## Residential Protected Room (Mamad) Maintenance

What must a residential protected room (Mamad) include?

The area of the room must be at least 9 square meters. In each protected room, a sealed blast door and window, electrical outlets, and communication outlets for phones and television, must be installed. Flooring and wall reinforcements in compliance with Standard 5075 must be installed. Since 2002, it has been mandatory to install pipelines for the passage of infrastructure such as air conditioning, and from May 2010, a ventilation and filtration system must be installed in a new construction, according to Israeli Standard 4570.

How to maintain the integrity of the Mamad?

In order for the residential protected room to best serve us in emergencies, it is important to refrain from dismantling the framework, or any parts of it, including the doors and windows. Additionally, it is forbidden to drill holes penetrating the protected room’s walls. If changes or modifications to the protected room have been made, it is highly recommended to return it to its original state at the earliest available opportunity. Also, pay attention to the following emphases:  
**Blast door:** make sure the door opens and closes easily (including the double lock for emergencies) that the handle can be turned 90 degrees to face upwards, that light does not penetrate the room and that there is rubber insulation surrounding the door (ensure that the insulation has not dried out).  
**Steel outer window:** make sure that the window can be opened and closed easily.  
**Glass inner window:** if a single-wing window is installed, make sure that the window can be opened and closed easily (most models have a double lock for emergencies).If a double-wing window is installed in the protected rooms (wing on wing type double-wing window) - it must be removed during times of emergency only.  
**Ventilation pipes (if present):** make sure there is rubber insulation and that the steel cover’s screws can be tightened all the way.  
**Protected room walls:** if there are shelves or heavy items attached to the walls of the protected room, it must be fixed to the wall. Do not hang glass or ceramic items, such as mirrors, that might shatter.  
**Storage:** flammable, dangerous and toxic materials must not be stored in the protected room. Please note: It is forbidden to install gas tanks in less than 3 meters from the walls of the protected room. If the gas cylinders are not installed in accordance with the regulations, contact the gas company to remove them and report to the local authority. [Use this link](https://www.oref.org.il/media/pojhgp5g/%D7%94%D7%A8%D7%97%D7%A7%D7%AA-%D7%91%D7%9C%D7%95%D7%A0%D7%99-%D7%92%D7%96-%D7%9E%D7%9E%D7%A8%D7%97%D7%91-%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%92%D7%9F.pdf "הרחקת בלוני גז ממרחב מוגן") to download an official letter from the Home Front Command regarding the necessity of installing the gas cylinders at least 3 meters away from the protected room walls.

What permits are required in order for a residential protected room to meet the standards?

Every protected room in the country requires a building permit and an occupancy permit. Without these permits, it is not permitted to reside in the building. For that reason, as a general rule, in any building which has received a building permit, the protected room has been tested and has met the standards before the apartment was handed over to the tenant. This is the responsibility of the local planning committee.

What are the permitted uses of the residential protected room?

The protected room may be used for any purpose, with the exception of kitchens, washrooms or bathrooms.  
The protected room can be used as a bedroom, but it must be ventilated.  
It is not recommended to use the protected room for storage purposes. It is forbidden to store flammable, dangerous and toxic materials in the protected room, and no glass or ceramic items that could shatter, like mirrors, can be hung.

Who can I contact to check the integrity of the protected room?

To check the intactness of the protected room’s structure - contact a certified engineer in order to formalize an estimation in regard to the Home Front Command regulations. To check the intactness of the protected room’s door and window’s frames and sealing or the communal shelter, contact a certified lab:

Who should be contacted if repairs to the protected rooms are needed?

The protected room is owned by the property owner, therefore, he is responsible for its maintenance and upkeeping. Use a certified professional, with experience in installing protected spaces. If repairs to the door or the window of the protected room are required, it is advised to contact the manufacturer, or to [one of the companies that manufacture frames](https://www.sii.org.il/he/certificationssearch?approveType=11&auto=true&query=4422) for protected rooms according to standards.

How can you check the blast door of the protected room?

Check that the door opens and closes properly (including the double lock for emergencies) when the door handle is turned 90 degrees, that light does not penetrate the room and that the rubber insulation is present (and has not dried out).

What is the standard thickness of a protected room door?

In each protected space, a door approved by the Standards Institute is installed. There are many types of protected room doors, determined according to the architectural configuration of the building, and their thickness varies accordingly. In any case - do not replace the original protected room door with another door. If the original blast door has been replaced, you can contact the manufacturer [or the company that manufactures the standard frame items for the shelters.](https://www.sii.org.il/he/certificationssearch?approveType=11&auto=true&query=4422)

Is it possible to replace the protected room door?

An interior door can be added to the door frame of the protected space for everyday use, but do not disassemble the protected room door.

How can we keep the protected room's window in good repair?

The protected room's window is a two-part structure that includes an outer steel window, and an inner glass window. Changes to the protected room's window are not allowed. In times of routine, the window's integrity must be checked as follows:

**Outer steel window** – if the steel window does no shut well, clear the dust from the window's tracks and pocket. If the steel window has been removed, install it back as soon as possible.  
**Inner glass window** – most protected rooms have a single-wing glass window that is designed to withstand blast. In order to make sure it is in working order, make sure it closes properly (usually it has a double emergency locking system). In addition, some protected rooms have a glass window built from two wings (double wing of the wing on wing type) that must be removed in emergency situations.

If a repair of the protected room's window is required - [contact one of the companies that manufacture frames for protected rooms according to standards.](https://www.sii.org.il/he/certificationssearch?approveType=11&auto=true&query=4422)

Why are there two windows in the residential protected room?

The two windows in the protected room have two different functions:  
**Steel outer window** - designed to prevent the penetration of blast and shrapnel.  
**Glass window** – most protected rooms have a single-wing glass window that is used to sealing and helps with reducing the intensity of blasts. If a single-wing window is installed – it must be closed during an emergency.  
In addition, in some protected rooms, a double-wing windows of wing on wing type is installed. In this case the outer steel window is also used for sealing. In protected rooms with a double-wing glass type window, the window should be removed during an emergency.

What types of external steel windows can be installed in the Protected Room (Mamad)?

The external steel window in the Protected Room (Mamad) is designed to prevent the penetration of the blast waves and shrapnel.  

There are four possible types of exterior steel window:

1. A single-winged steel window, pulled out of a pocket in the wall.  
2. A double-winged steel window, meaning that it consists of two wings, each of which is pulled out of a pocket. In this way, the window is less heavy.  
3. A double-winged window, meaning that it consists of two wings, with both wings opening outwards.  
4. Single-wing steel window, having the wing opens outwards.

Is it allowed to install a mosquito net in the protected room window?

Yes. There is a rail set especially for the net on the frame. During an emergency, you must remove the net.

Is it allowed to install bars in the protected room window?

It is allowed to install bars (closed and opening) in the protected room window, as long as the bars are installed outside the steel window and do not affect its use.

Is it allowed to install shutters in the protected room window?

It is allowed to install shutters in the protected room window, as long as they are installed outside the steel window and do not affect its use.

Is it allowed to drill the walls of the protected room?

According to regulations, it is not allowed to drill the walls of a protected room. Pipework should be installed only in an approved piping passage as detailed [in this link](https://www.oref.org.il/media/l13puo0b/%D7%9E%D7%A2%D7%91%D7%A8%D7%99-%D7%A6%D7%A0%D7%A8%D7%AA-%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%93%D7%95%D7%9C%D7%95%D7%A8%D7%99%D7%99%D7%9D-%D7%94%D7%9E%D7%90%D7%95%D7%A9%D7%A8%D7%99%D7%9D-%D7%9C%D7%A9%D7%99%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%A9.pdf "מעברי צנרת מודולוריים המאושרים לשימוש").

Is it allowed to hang shelves, TVs, pictures and mirrors in the protected room?

It is allowed to install cabinets, shelves and a TV in the protected room, but only on an internal well. The installation must be carried out according to manufacturer's instructions and make sure they are firmly fixed to the wall. Do not hang ceramic or glass items (such as mirrors) that could shatter.

Is it possible to store equipment in a protected room?

it is not recommended to use the protected room as a storage room. It is forbidden to store flammable, dangerous and toxic materials in the protected room. If there are cabinets and shelves in the protected room, they should be fixed to the walls. Do not hang ceramic or glass items, such as mirros, that could shatter.

Is it allowed to cover the ventilation openings in the protected room?

Routinely, it is recommended not to.

It is allowed to install a cabinet covering the protected room's ventilation system?

A cabinet should not be placed over the entry pipe of the ventilation opening, which is used to let air into the protected room and connects to the filtering system.

A cabinet can be placed over the release pipe opening (a pipe letting the air out of the protected room), and also over the air conditioning piper opening (a pipe used for the air conditioner), making sure that the cabinet is not tight to the wall, or creating an opening in the cabinet over these openings.

Is there a requirement to install a filtration system in the protected room?

Yes, if it was granted a building permit after 18.05.2010.

What are the guidelines for installing an air conditioner in a protected room?

Air conditioning must be installed in the protected room according to Israeli Standard 994 Part 5.   
An air conditioner may only be installed on the inner wall of the protected room.   
The air conditioner piping must be routed through a modular/industrial passageway with a valid permit from the Home Front Command.

Is it permissible to whitewash the walls of the MMAD or the MMAK?

The walls of the protected space can be whitewashed in accordance with the requirements of Israeli Standard 5075.

Is it possible to cover the walls and door of the protected room?

Walls can only be covered in accordance with Standard 5075.  
Do not make any changes to the blast door of the protected room.  
The lightweight inner door of the protected room can be covered provided the cladding meets fire resistance requirements.

What cladding and flooring can be done in a protected room?

Cladding and flooring must be carried out in accordance with Standard 5075. Verify with the installer that he meets the standard.

Does the protected room standard require the installation of a communication point?

It is mandatory to connect points for telephone and TV in the protected room. It is recommended to install the TV on an internal wall and not drill from side to side.

Is it allowed to install a ceiling fan in a protected room?

A ceiling fan is not included in the elements permitted for installation within the framework applicable to the ceiling of the protected room, and its installation may endanger its occupants in the event of a rocket and missile attack.

What is the minimum distance between gas cylinders and a protected space?

Three meters. If the gas cylinders are not installed in accordance with the regulations, contact the gas company to remove them and report to the local authority. [Use this link to download an official letter from the Home Front Command regarding the obligation to install gas cylinders at least 3 meters from the shelter walls](https://www.oref.org.il/media/smhjwmzf/whatsapp-image-2025-07-13-at-143131-1-1.pdf "Whatsapp Image 2025 07 13 At 14.31.31 (1) (1)").

It is allowed to install a washing machine in the protected room?

It is not recommended to install a washing machine in the protected room, but it is possible to do so, as long as the pipework for water and drainage is installed though the approved piping passages and subject to a permit for the piping passages provided by the Home Front Command.

Is it mandatory to enter the protected room directly or can you enter from another room?

There is no restriction on entering the protected room from another room in the house.

What is the standard width of the outer steel window of the protected room?

Every protected rooms has a widow approved by the Israeli Standards Authority. In most parts of the country, the thickness of the protected room's steel window is 18 mm, except for towns near the border in the north and in the Gaza Envelope, where the requirement in new buildings since 2018 is for 24 mm thick steel. In the Gaza Envelope, the requirement since 2021 is 32 mm.

To which buildings in the Gaza Envelope does the recommendation to thicken protected room windows apply – new buildings, or also existing buildings?

The new standard for protected room windows in the Gaza Envelope applies only for new residential and public buildings. The Home Front Command recommended the Government to fund a project for increasing the thickness of the protected room windows in existing buildings, but a government decision is required to implement such a project.

Will the Government fund the thickening of protected room windows in existing builds in the Gaza Envelope?

The Home Front Command recommended the Government to fund a project for increasing the thickness of the protected room windows in existing buildings, but a Government decision on the extent of the funding is required for such a project. As of now, residents can improve the windows using their own funds.

Why does the recommendation to increase thickness of protected room windows apply only to a 7 KM range from the Gaza Strip border?

The recommendation to update the Standard for thickness of protected room windows in the Gaza Envelope area to 32 mm was given based on risk assessment. Therefore, the new Standard only applies to new constructions in towns defined as frontier towns in the Civilian Safety Commander Order in the Southern zone only.

Is it possible to install a protected room steel window thicker than the Standard requires?

The details of frameworks in protected rooms are set in the Israeli Standard 4422. The Standard sets a minimal thickness for safety windows. If the property owner is interested in installing in their home a safety window with a thicker steel plate, it is possible and depends on their decision.

Has the Home Front Command tested the 32 mm thick steel window to ensure it withstands the threat in the south?

The update of the Standard to a 32 mm thick steel sheet, as recommended by the Home Front Command, significantly improved the ability of the protected space to withstand threats in the Southern zone.

Are there instructions and specifications for a 32 mm steel window?

The details of protected space frames are set by the Israeli Standard 4422.

Can flammable materials be taken into the residential protected room?

No flammable materials should be taken into the residential protected room.
