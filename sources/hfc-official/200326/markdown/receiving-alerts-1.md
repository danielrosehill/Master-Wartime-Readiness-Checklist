## preliminary guideline

What is a preliminary guideline?

As part of the headquarters work carried out by the Home Front Command, from now on a preliminary guideline will be received on the Home Front Command App about a rocket and missile attack coming from from a distant hostile source towards the territory of the State of Israel, approximately 3-5 minutes before receiving the alert. This is in order to enable the preparation and readiness of citizens to reach the best protection in their area.

Why was it decided to provide a preliminary guideline about a rocket and missile attack from Yemen?

When the missile is launched remotely, it allows, among other things, the ability of the preliminary guideline, which is intended to provide citizens in the threatened area with time to prepare to reach the best protection in their area, even before receiving the alert guiding them to enter the protected space.

Why is there no preliminary guideline for rocket and missile attack from Gaza or Lebanon?

A preliminary guideline is designed to prepare the population in relevant areas for the possibility of an alert when a rocket or missile is launched from a sufficiently distant source, providing us with enough time to identify and issue a warning in advance.

An all-clear notification and instruction to exit the protected space will still be sent in the event of an attack from Gaza or Lebanon.

Why is the time between a preliminary guideline and an alert sometimes so short?

The Home Front Command issues a preliminary guideline for mobile devices in relevant areas as early as possible to help you move toward a protected space. Occasionally, the preliminary guideline arrives relatively close to the alert. When you get an alert, you will need to immediately enter the protected space within the time available to reach shelter.

Why did I receive an alert only, without a preliminary guideline?

A preliminary guideline is given a few minutes before receiving an alert. Its purpose is to allow additional time to improve your position and reach the optimal protected space in your area. This guideline will be given whenever possible only in areas where the shooting comes from remote locations.

When the shooting comes from nearby areas, an alert only will be given according to the time available to reach shelter.

The alert will be received through a variety means of alert: the siren system, the Home Front Command app, the National Emergency Portal, and supplementary platforms: Telegram, television, and radio.

Please note: if a preliminary guideline was received, and an alert follows immediately after, you must enter the protected space within your time available to reach shelter and not leave until you receive an explicit guideline.

How will the preliminary guideline about a rocket and missile attack from Yemen be received?

The preliminary guideline will be received via the Home Front Command app in areas under threat based on the location and areas of interest that were defined, by an update sound (without a rising and falling alert) and by a notification message on the mobile phone, which will read that "in the coming minutes, alerts are expected in your area." In addition, the guideline will be received on the National Emergency Portal (this website) and on the Home Front Command's Telegram channels.

How far in advance will the preliminary guideline about a rocket and missile attack from Yemen be received?

The preliminary guideline will be received a few minutes before receiving the alert guiding us to enter the protected space.

Will the preliminary guideline about a rocket and missile attack be received in a targeted manner, or will it be distributed throughout the country?

The preliminary guideline will be received in the Home Front Command App only in areas where there is a threat potential according to the device's self-location and according to the area of interest defined in the App. In addition, the preliminary guideline will also be received on the National Emergency Portal according to the areas of interest defined, and on the Home Front Command Telegram channels.

What should we do if we receive a preliminary guideline about a ricket and missile attack?

This time can be used to advance preparations and readiness to receiving the alert, for example by choosing a better protected space, ensuring that the window and door of the protected room (Mamad) are closed, or postponing leaving home or work. Only if after receiving the preliminary guideline, an alert is received, you need to enter a protected space in accordance with the time available to reach shelter and stay there.

I received a preliminary guideline about a rocket and missile attack Should I enter the protected space?

The preliminary guideline does not require entering a protected space but is intended to give time and additional information for advance alertness and preparedness to citizens to ensure better protection, as well as to improve their sense of personal security. Only if an alert is received in your area, you need to enter the protected space in accordance to the time available to reach shelter.

Why did we receive a preliminary guideline about a rocket and missile attack but did not receive an alert afterwards?

The preliminary guideline is distributed to large areas where there is a potential threat to allow time for preparation, while the alert is distributed to areas where the threat is realized. Therefore, there may be cases in which the preliminary guideline will be distributed to larger areas than the areas where an alert will be activated.

Why was it decided only now to provide citizens with a preliminary guideline of a rocket and missile attack from Yemen?

The preliminary guideline is made possible as part of headquarters work carried out by the Home Front Command, with the aim of enabling the preparation and readiness of citizens to reach the best protection near them.

In the event of a preliminary guideline of rocket and missile fire - what should I do if I am not at home?

It is important to take advantage of the time in order to ensure that you can enter the best possible protected space upon receipt of an alert. Therefore, one must calculate the travel time, keep abreast of traffic jams and congestion, and accordingly make the best decision. If you estimate that you will not be able to reach your home, you should remain in place and, upon receipt of an alert or directive from the Home Front Command, enter the most protected space in your vicinity.

We received a preliminary guideline about a rocket and missile attack but did not receive an alert afterwards. Should we enter a protected space?

If no alert has been received in your area after receiving the preliminary guideline - you are not required to complete the action of entering the protected space.

We have a common shelter that can be reached within a few minutes. If we receive a preliminary guideline, do we have to go to the shelter?

If you don’t have a protected room (Mamad), and you have a shelter that you can reach - when receiving a preliminary guideline you may go to the shelter.

What should we do if a preliminary guideline is received while driving?

If a nearby building can be reached within a few minutes - you can continue driving and reach the building.

How one should act if a preliminary guideline is received while on the street on in an open area?

Find a better place and reach the best protection available in your area

What should we do if a preliminary guideline is received while our children are playing in the street?

You can call the children to come home or inform them that they must go into a nearby building.

What should we do if a preliminary guideline is received exactly when we planned to go out for a drive/take a shower?

You should wait with the action you intended to take until the end of the event.

How will the educational staff act if a preliminary guideline is received during the school day?

The educational staff will act in accordance with the Ministry of Education guidelines and have been briefed on how to act if a preliminary guideline is received, to allow students to improve their protection.

What should be done if a preliminary guideline is received while on a student transportation?

If a preliminary guideline is received before the transportation has set off - the students will be able to return with their parents to their homes or enter a nearby building in order to improve their protection. If the students are already on the transportation bus on their way - the driver will prepare to stop in a safe place, and upon receiving an alert, the students will bend below the window line.

What should be done if a preliminary guideline is received while traveling by bus?

The driver will act to stop the bus safely on the side of the road or in a place that allows passengers to reach the best location in their area. Upon receiving an alert, the driver will act in accordance with the guidelines for traveling by public transportation.

What should be done if a preliminary guideline is received while traveling by train?

The train will continue its journey. If a rocket and missile attack alert is received, the driver will slow down to 30 km/h and passengers will be asked to bend below the window line.

How can people without a smartphone receive the preliminary guideline?

The IDF spokesman will distribute a message in the media stating that a rocket and missile attack is suspected towards the State of Israel in the coming minutes, and that it is required to follow the Home Front Command guidelines. This message will allow for a broad update, but not in a selective manner.  
It should be emphasized that a preliminary guideline is intended to prepare the population for the possibility of receiving an alert as a result of a rocket and missile attack, but it does not replace the Home Front Command alerts that are received in areas where the threat is real and are distributed on sirens and various platforms.

How should one act if a preliminary guideline or an alert is received during student transportation?

If a preliminary guideline is received during the journey and there are several minutes to reach a nearby standard protected space, the driver will prepare to stop in a safe location and the students must exit the bus and reach the standard protected space.  
If a standard protected space cannot be reached, the driver will carefully stop in a safe place at the side of the road and open the transportation vehicle's doors, and upon receiving the alert (siren), the students will remain in their places and bend below the window line.  
In the Central Command (Pakmaz) only - the driver will continue driving until reaching a safe place.
