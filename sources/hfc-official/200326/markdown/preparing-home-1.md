## Preparing the protected space and equipment, securing objects and planning a safe escape route are a few steps that should be taken routinely to ensure the home is the safest place for us. Here's the full list

In emergencies, such as a rocket and missile attack, an earthquake, extreme weather, a fire or a hazardous materials incident, preparing your physical environment has a crucial influence on your chances of surviving the event.

Home is where we have the most control of preparedness. In order for it to be the safest place for us, we need to prepare it during routine. How can we do that?

‎1. **Prepare the protected space** – [choose the protected space](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/articles/info/preparing-protected-space/1200/ "הנחיות לבחירת המרחב המוגן") and prepare it including [emergency equipment](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/articles/info/preparing-protected-space/1201/ "ציוד לשעת חירום") and methods of communication, securing cabinets and shelves, verifying the room is clear of flammable, hazardous or toxic materials glass or porcelain items that may shatter, such as mirrors. [For information on preparing the protected room for emergency](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/articles/info/preparing-protected-space/1205)

2. **Secure objects -** any object that is likely to move, fall over or break pose a risk during of an emergency. To lower risk, do the following:

● Secure bookcases and shelves to the wall.

● Lock the wheels of heavy household appliances (washing machines, refrigerators, etc.)

● Ensure heavy objects are placed in low positions. Do not place heavy or fragile objects in a high position (on shelves, bookcases, etc.)

● Identify additional danger areas in your home and treat them. For example, we will not place porcelain or glass objects in the protected space, and on the way to it, we will avoid placing heavy objects near windows, etc.

**3. Keep flammable or toxic materials, such as paint or cleaning supplies, in closed vessels and away from heat sources.**

**‎4. Gather the family’s emergency supplies** and store them in the protected space at home, preferably in one bag. Ensure that all members of your family know each of the items and how and when to use the equipment.  
[For information on preparing family supplies for emergencies.](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/articles/info/preparing-protected-space/1201/ "ציוד לשעת חירום")

**5. Check the intactness of electrical, gas, and fuel connections.** Make sure all family members know where the gas and electric points are located in the house, and under which circumstances should the faucets be shut off - and how to do so, in order for them to shut them off in case of an emergency.

**6. Plan the safest possible escape route in case you need to evacuate your home immediately.** Take into account doors and openings that are not used on a daily basis, such as back doors, porches, and emergency staircases. As such, we will make sure the family members know these routes and that they are clear of obstacles, portable objects that may block them, and of flammable materials.

**7. Keep a contact list in case of emergencies in a prominent place.** [For a list of the phone numbers that should be kept for emergency situations, press here.](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/articles/info/preparing-protected-space/1211)

## Q&A

Residential Protected Room (Mamad) Maintenance

What must a residential protected room (Mamad) include?

The area of the room must be at least 9 square meters. In each protected room, a sealed blast door and window, electrical outlets, and communication outlets for phones and television, must be installed. Flooring and wall reinforcements in compliance with Standard 5075 must be installed. Since 2002, it has been mandatory to install pipelines for the passage of infrastructure such as air conditioning, and from May 2010, a ventilation and filtration system must be installed in a new construction, according to Israeli Standard 4570.

How to maintain the integrity of the Mamad?

In order for the residential protected room to best serve us in emergencies, it is important to refrain from dismantling the framework, or any parts of it, including the doors and windows. Additionally, it is forbidden to drill holes penetrating the protected room’s walls. If changes or modifications to the protected room have been made, it is highly recommended to return it to its original state at the earliest available opportunity. Also, pay attention to the following emphases:  
**Blast door:** make sure the door opens and closes easily (including the double lock for emergencies) that the handle can be turned 90 degrees to face upwards, that light does not penetrate the room and that there is rubber insulation surrounding the door (ensure that the insulation has not dried out).  
**Steel outer window:** make sure that the window can be opened and closed easily.  
**Glass inner window:** if a single-wing window is installed, make sure that the window can be opened and closed easily (most models have a double lock for emergencies).If a double-wing window is installed in the protected rooms (wing on wing type double-wing window) - it must be removed during times of emergency only.  
**Ventilation pipes (if present):** make sure there is rubber insulation and that the steel cover’s screws can be tightened all the way.  
**Protected room walls:** if there are shelves or heavy items attached to the walls of the protected room, it must be fixed to the wall. Do not hang glass or ceramic items, such as mirrors, that might shatter.  
**Storage:** flammable, dangerous and toxic materials must not be stored in the protected room. Please note: It is forbidden to install gas tanks in less than 3 meters from the walls of the protected room. If the gas cylinders are not installed in accordance with the regulations, contact the gas company to remove them and report to the local authority. [Use this link](https://www.oref.org.il/media/pojhgp5g/%D7%94%D7%A8%D7%97%D7%A7%D7%AA-%D7%91%D7%9C%D7%95%D7%A0%D7%99-%D7%92%D7%96-%D7%9E%D7%9E%D7%A8%D7%97%D7%91-%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%92%D7%9F.pdf "הרחקת בלוני גז ממרחב מוגן") to download an official letter from the Home Front Command regarding the necessity of installing the gas cylinders at least 3 meters away from the protected room walls.

What permits are required in order for a residential protected room to meet the standards?

Every protected room in the country requires a building permit and an occupancy permit. Without these permits, it is not permitted to reside in the building. For that reason, as a general rule, in any building which has received a building permit, the protected room has been tested and has met the standards before the apartment was handed over to the tenant. This is the responsibility of the local planning committee.

What are the permitted uses of the residential protected room?

The protected room may be used for any purpose, with the exception of kitchens, washrooms or bathrooms.  
The protected room can be used as a bedroom, but it must be ventilated.  
It is not recommended to use the protected room for storage purposes. It is forbidden to store flammable, dangerous and toxic materials in the protected room, and no glass or ceramic items that could shatter, like mirrors, can be hung.

Who can I contact to check the integrity of the protected room?

To check the intactness of the protected room’s structure - contact a certified engineer in order to formalize an estimation in regard to the Home Front Command regulations. To check the intactness of the protected room’s door and window’s frames and sealing or the communal shelter, contact a certified lab:

* For a list of labs for testing protective frames (for testing the integrity of the blast door and windows in the protected room): use [this link](https://www.israc.gov.il/?NameOrMisLab=-1&AuthorizationStatus=&Technology=-1&SearchFree=SI+4422&pg=searchLab&CategoryID=231&PostBack=1) to search standard "SI 4422”.
* ⁠For a list of labs for testing airtightness (for testing the protected room's resistance to blast): use [this link](https://www.israc.gov.il/?NameOrMisLab=-1&AuthorizationStatus=&Technology=-1&SearchFree=SI+4577&pg=searchLab&CategoryID=231&PostBack=1) to search standard "SI 4577".
