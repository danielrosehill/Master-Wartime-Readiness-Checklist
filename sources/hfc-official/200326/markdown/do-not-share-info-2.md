## Rumors and ‘fake news’ can negatively affect many, especially during emergency situations. Therefore, it is important to obtain information only from official and trustworthy sources.

Operation "Rising Lion" placed the Israeli home front in the face of an Iranian attack that included the launching of missiles and hostile aircraft against civilian settlements and national infrastructure. In the face of the threats, the public's need for timely and up-to-date information is growing – but precisely at these hours, each and every one of us has a personal responsibility to refrain from disseminating false information ("fake news") and rumors about injuries and casualties, which may cause real damage – both to the security of the state and to the public's well-being.

To maintain the routine of our lives, it is of the utmost importance to consume information only from official and reliable sources, and to be wary of accepting information from other sources. Official sources are those that disseminate reliable information, which has been checked and approved by professional authorities prior to its dissemination, such as official publications of governmental bodies, and information from institutions, organizations and services authorized to present information, such as the IDF, police, fire and rescue services, medical services and local municipalities.

**Key points for conveying information in an emergency**  
To avoid transmitting rumors and unfounded information, which can cause harm to others,  
we should ask ourselves three questions before we transmit information online:  
Was the information received from a reliable source?  
Could the information hurt someone?  
Could the information be useful to others?  
If the answers raise any red flags, think twice before sharing information.

### Providing warnings, instructions and information in emergency situations

The Home Front Command is tasked with disseminating alerts to citizens when they are in danger, and guides them on how they should act by providing life-saving guidelines to the population and setting policies for managing the life routine. The Home Front Command’s guidelines and other important information can be obtained through the following means:  

**Home Front Command representatives** – In an emergency situation, the Home Front Command's public representatives provide the Home Front Command’s guidelines and life-saving information in all the media. Each media channel, each sector and each language have a dedicated Home Front Command representative.  

**The Home Front Command's pages on social networks** –[Facebook](https://www.facebook.com/PikudHaoref), [WhatsApp](https://www.whatsapp.com/channel/0029VaGnhO57oQhig33jiH31), [Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/pikudhaoref/), [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCaohhNZVh3zq42wxumOZq1A), [Telegram](https://t.me/PikudHaOref_all) and [X (Twitter](https://x.com/PikudHaoref1)) also provide information about life-saving guidelines, guidelines for managing the routine and additional information.  

**Local Municipalities** – The Home Front Command conveys the guidelines to the local municipalities, for the purpose of distribution and updating the civilians.

**Harmful rumors and ‘fake news’**  
In an emergency situation, rumors channel distress resulting from uncertainty and lack of information in a way that creates the illusion of control over the situation. Spreading rumors helps some people to overcome mental distress, for example, by finding partners to their concerns, or hoping that others will dispel the rumor and calm the fears. Sometimes it meets needs such as wish to be the center of attention and achieve a sense of controlling the mindset.

It is important to remember that rumors can increase panic, fear and pressure on the individual, the family and the community, and impair the ability to cope effectively with the situation. On the other hand, rumors may cause complacence, leading the public to refrain from taking the necessary actions to deal with the emergency situation.

Even positive rumors that weren't prechecked can be harmful. They express wishful thinking at times when it is difficult to bear reality, and try to embellish it. When it turns out that a positive rumor is not true, people who hold it to be true are discouraged, their trust in reliable information is undermined. Rumors can flourish even when the tension subsides, rekindling the tension and excitement because of a difficulty to return to the normal, dim routine.

Whether positive or negative, the damage in rumors is the distortion of reality and an impairment in the ability to deal with it. It is important to explain the phenomenon, without justifying or encouraging it. The urge to share with others a rumor we hear is natural and normal, and it is important to be aware of this so that our behavior is more contained, and controlled. For example, by finding legitimate ways to satisfy the need for information, such as joint reading of written information from authorized sources.

## Q&A

Means of Alert

How can I receive an alert according to my exact location?

The Home Front Command app enables you to receive an alert on your smartphone according to your exact location (make sure to enable location access on your phone). This way wherever you are, the app will recognise your location and send an alert if necessary.

How can I receive the Home Command alerts?

Home Front Command alerts are provided in a various means of alert, in the following ways:

1.⁠ ⁠Personal – Home Front Command app, through which you can receive personal alerts wherever you are in up to 10 additional areas of interest, [to download the app click here](https://onelink.to/8p5wpq)

2.**⁠ ⁠**Home – The National Emergency Portal on your home computer allows you to receive a targeted and personal alert to your home whenever an alert is activated in your town, provided that the website is open on the computer. [For a detailed explanation, click here.](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/articles/info/iron-swords/1109/ "אמצעי ההתרעה של פיקוד העורף")

3.**⁠ ⁠**Public – Home Front Command sirens deployed throughout the country and intended mainly for those outside the building. The sirens provide an alert via a rising and falling alarm or a red alarm in the Gaza Envelope area, and its duration is the duration of the time it takes to reach the protected space in that area.

**⁠In addition, Home Front Command alerts are also received through the following media:**  

•⁠ ⁠National and regional **radio stations**.  
•⁠ **⁠Television channels** – Kan Broadcasting Corporation 11, Keshet Channel 12, Reshet Channel 13, Channel 14, Channel 9 and Channel 140.  
•⁠ **⁠Websites** – Ynet, Mako, Reshet, Israel Hayom, Walla and Channel 7.  

In addition, you can download the Home Front Command app for TV, to receive real-time alerts when watching any content on TVs running the Android TV operating system.   
[For more details](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/articles/info/iron-swords/1119/ "יישומון פיקוד העורף בטלוויזיה")

How can I receive an alert in the National Emergency Portal?

The National Emergency Portal allows you to receive focused alerts on a variety of threats to your home. To receive real-time alerts, turn on the sound alerts by clicking the speaker icon next to the Alerts tab on the home page, and then define the name of the town you wish to receive alerts for, or an exact address if your city is divided into several alert zones, on the Alerts page. You can define up to 10 areas for which you will receive an alert (if you do not define areas, you will receive an alert for the whole country). **Please note: In order for the home alert to work, the National Emergency Portal needs to be open on your computer.**

Why did I receive an alert saying "In your area" instead of my town's name? What should I do?

The Home Front Command app is a personal alert system that sends a notification based on a specific location. There are cases where residents living near open areas might receive a personal alert on the app even if a siren hasn't been activated for the entire town.  
Additionally, in areas where small towns are clustered closely together, the app may recognize the general vicinity as a single zone, resulting in an alert that says "In your area".  
In any case, if you receive an alert through any of the Home Front Command’s official channels, you must enter your protected space immediately.

How can I receive alerts on my TV?

When inside your home, you may receive alerts on TV; the alert will appear as subtitles on the right of your screen indicating the names of the authorities - for example, Ramla, Lod, etc. In a city which has been subdivided, the name of the zone which is subject to the alert will be displayed: for example, Be’er Sheva South. Television alerts are aired on channels: 11, 12, 13,14, 9 and 140.

In addition, you can download the Home Front Command App for TV, to be able to receive real-time alerts while watching all contents operating on an Android TV operating system. [Press here for more information](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/articles/info/iron-swords/1119/ "חדש! יישומון פיקוד העורף בטלוויזיה")
