## Life- Saving Guidelines During a Hazardous Materials Event

How should we act if staying indoors during a hazardous materials event?

It is required to stay indoors, close the doors and windows and turn off the air conditioner. Do not leave the building until you receive a message the event has ended.

Should I evacuate from the my house during a hazardous materials event?

Do not leave the house if there is risk that hazardous materials spread in your area. Staying inside with doors and windows closed, protects you and prevents exposure to hazardous materials carried through the air. It is required to turn off the air conditioner.

What should I do if I am outdoors during a Hazardous Materials Event?

Immediately enter a building, close the doors and windows, and turn off the air conditioner if it is a fixed in the window. Do not leave the building until you receive a message the event has ended. If it is not possible to enter a building - stay as far away from the area of event as possible.

What should I do if I am in a vehicle during a Hazardous Materials Event?

You must stop safely at the side of the road, close the windows, and turn off the air conditioner.

What are the guidelines for people staying close to the area where a Hazardous Materials Event has occurred?

No special action should be taken unless instructed to do so by the Home Front Command. Only when receiving an alert and guideline from the Home Front Command on a hazardous materials event, should one stay secluded indoors, close windows and doors, turn off the air conditioner if it is fixed in the window, and wait for further guidelines.

What do we do if we receive an alert of a rocket and missile attack during a Hazardous Materials Event?

If you receive an alert of a rocket and missile attack during a hazardous materials event - enter the protected room or an inner room and stay there for 10 minutes. You can leave the protected room or inner room after 10 minutes, but continue to stay secluded in your home space with your doors and windows closed, until you receive a message the event has ended.   
During a hazardous materials event, do not go out into a protected space located outside your home (including a Mamak - floor protected space, Mamam – institutional protected space, stairwell or shelter), as this can expose us to the hazardous material carried in the air.

How does staying indoors protect us during a Hazardous Materials Event?

When there is a risk of hazardous material spreading, avoid exposure to outdoor air as much as possible. Therefore, staying secluded indoors, including closing the doors and windows, protects us and prevents the dangerous material carried in the air from penetrating.

Why can’t the stairwell be used as a protected space, if we receive an alert of a rocket and missile attack during a Hazardous Materials Event?

The stairwell is not sealed enough and therefore cannot protect you from the hazardous material carried in the air. Therefore, if you receive an alert of a rocket and missile attack during a hazardous materials event, enter the protected room or an inner room and stay there. You can leave the protected room or inner room, but continue to stay secluded in your home space with your doors and windows closed, until you receive a message the event has ended.

Why can’t we use the Mamak or shelter as a protected space, if we receive an alert of a rocket and missile attack during a Hazardous Materials Event?

Going to a Mamak or shelter exposes you to the hazardous materials carried in the air. Therefore, if you receive an alert of a rocket and missile attack during a hazardous materials event, you must enter the protected room (Mamad) or an inner room and stay there. You can leave the protected room or inner room, but continue to stay secluded in your home space with your doors and windows closed, until you receive a message the event has ended.

Can the protective kit help in a Hazardous Materials Event?

The protective kit is not suitable for all the various materials available. When there is a risk of hazardous material spreading, avoid exposure to outdoor air as much as possible. Therefore, staying secluded indoors, including closing the doors and windows and turning off the air conditioner if it is fixed in the window, provides a life-saving solution.

Is it possible to turn on the air conditioner during a Hazardous Materials Event?

When there is a risk of hazardous material spreading, avoid exposure to outdoor air as much as possible. An air conditioner that is fixed in the window brings air from outside into the building and allows hazardous materials to enter the house, therefore you should not turn it on. You can operate an air conditioner that is not fixed in the window and does not let air in from outside.

Is it possible to activate the ventilation and filtration system during a Hazardous Materials Event?

When there is a risk of hazardous material spreading, avoid exposure to outdoor air as much as possible. An external ventilation system lets air from outside into the building and is able to filter only some of the hazardous materials. Therefore, it might bring some of the hazardous materials into the structure when it is operating, thus, it should not be turned on.

How can I differentiate between a rocket and missile attack alert and a Hazardous Materials alert?

Unlike a rocket and missile attack alert, in which you will only hear the sound of an alarm, in a hazardous materials event alert, you will hear a short alarm sound followed by the announcement: "Hazardous Materials". This way you can immediately differentiate between the threats and act accordingly to protect yourself in real time.

Where can I get updates on the end of a Hazardous Materials Event?

Information about the end of the event will be transferred on Home Front Command platforms - the National Emergency Portal, the Home Front Command app, official social media pages and the media.

An alert was received about a potential leak of hazardous materials immediately after a rocket and missile attack alert was received. I am in the protected room (Mamad) and did not close the window at home. Should I leave the protected room (Mamad) to close it?

In case there is leak fear of hazardous materials while citizens are required to remain inside their protected spaces - the guideline is to remain inside the protected spaces until further notice, make sure the door and windows are tightly closed, and turn off the air conditioner.
