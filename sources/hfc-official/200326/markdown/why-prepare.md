## When we prepare for emergencies in advance, we can act correctly in real time and save ourselves and those around us. What emergencies threaten us and how can we prepare in advance?

Various dangers and emergencies, such as war, terrorism, cyber, hazardous materials and natural disasters, may strike us without warning. Experience shows that people who prepared and knew how to deal with various emergencies were able to act correctly and save themselves, their family members and those around

them. Therefore, the best time to prepare is today, in routine.  
The preparatory actions are few, simple and life-saving. The Home Front Command guides the public in the State of Israel, on an ongoing and continuous basis, through various means, on how to prepare for various emergencies. [This link contains highlights for individuals and for families on preparing for the various emergencies.](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/articles/emergency-preparedness/preparing-routine/2102/ "הכנת תוכנית חירום משפחתית")

### **What is an emergency?**

An emergency is a crisis caused by an external event that disrupts daily life. It is characterized by unusual and extreme needs, usually appears suddenly, threatens one or many people, causes concern of damage or serious danger and requires a speedy response.

An emergency situation is characterized by an imbalance between the situation requiring us to adapt, and the tools we have to deal with the obstacles we face. In this situation, the usual problem solving methods are not efficient, and the skills required to deal with the situations are not know, available or adequately accessible.

For example, if there is a situation in which an alert is received during an activity in an open space, people who are unfamiliar with guidelines on how to act during an alert, could react instinctively and run to look for a protected space. This puts them at greater risk in case a rocket falls nearby and can also put other people in danger in cases of crowds and possible stampedes. In this case, the correct reaction is to [lie on the ground, protecting your head with your hands.](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/articles/info/iron-swords/1105/)

### Mass emergencies vs. common emergencies

It is customary to distinguish between two types of emergencies:

**Mass emergencies –** pose a threat to a large section of the public, for example rocket and missile attacks or natural disasters such as earthquakes.

The way we act in the first moments when such situations occur has a deciding effect on our chances of saving ourselves and others.

Also, security forces, emergency officials, and rescue services may have difficulty in quickly reaching every impacted area, and we may need to deal with the situation on our own for an extended period of time.

This is why it’s important to prepare in advance, so that we know how to act during an emergency situation, and that we will have the necessary tools and information to protect ourselves and our loved ones.

**Personal emergencies –** are more limited in scope, but at times still have serious consequences in terms of physical or psychological damage. For example, a traffic accident, an electrical short that causes a fire, a work accident, and so on. An individual’s ability to save himself and the people around him through immediate action is critical in these situations.

### **The Threats on the State of Israel**

**The Security Threat**

Since its establishment, the State of Israel has been exposed to a wide range of threats from enemy states and terrorist organizations, whose goal is to hurt its strategic sites. The Home Front has become another main front, due to the enemy's focus on attempting to harm the civilian population in various ways, for example by [firing rockets and missiles](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/life-saving-guidelines/rocket-and-missile-attacks/).

The threat, as we have become familiar with it to date, is based on the concept that high-trajectory weapons will be launched over time at population centers in the Home Front of the State of Israel with the aim of exhausting it in a way that will prevent the State of Israel from achieving a clear and quick victory over the enemy (high-trajectory shooting is a type of attack that is carried out at a high angle while calculating the trajectory of the rocket or missile, and not in direct aiming where the shooter sees the target through the sight of the firing tool). [Click here for life-saving guidelines when firing rockets and missiles.](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/life-saving-guidelines/rocket-and-missile-attacks/)

The threat posed to the population could emerge from war or terror situations. We will now show the differences:

**War** - a war is an armed conflict between two sides whose goals are not aligned, and resort to an armed conflict to achieve a resolution. A war can last days, weeks, months and even more.

Legally, a war is a situation when the state’s legislators announce it formally, either through acts (such as committing an act of hostility against the other side). In war, using weaponry equiped with war heads such as bombs, artillery, rockets or unmanned aerial vehicles (UAV). These weapons are considered legal by the UN and are permitted to use during wartime.

**Terror** - the most substantial threat to the Home Front of the State of Israel today derives from the acts of terror organizations - a type of warfare done by organized groups or independents against an independent entity, that includes the use of force or violent acts, against the law and against international treaties, usually against civilians, in a goal to create political or social changes.

Terrorist acts systematically and continuously inflict terror and fear on an innocent and defenseless civilian population, through shooting, planting explosive devices, kidnapping and murdering people, and more. Terrorist organizations use terrorism as a means of extortion to achieve their goals, in violation of all international laws and treaties.

Today, terrorism constitutes an international threat and is defined as the greatest and most significant threat of all. It is possible to point out a number of common elements of terrorism, such as the use of violence or force, a political goal, instilling fear, threats and the application of psychological pressure, systematic or planned activity, and more.

**Cyber**

The rapid development of technology, along with the growing dependence on it, has led in recent years to a new threat that affects all areas of life - the cyber threat. Today, many means are controlled and supported by information systems, for example, water and electricity systems, cell phones, and the means of transportation we use (such as traffic lights, smart cars, and aviation). This fact poses a new technological challenge to the State of Israel, similar to all countries in the world.

Behind cyber attacks stand a variety of hostile entities: states and entities they support, terrorist organizations, criminal groups, activists, and individuals. The motivations and motives of the attackers are diverse, including harming national security, espionage and terrorism, crime, information theft, business competition, causing damage, influence and awareness, and various private goals.

Cyber security is critical to every action, service and process in the daily life of everyone, from the ordinary citizen to the critical infrastructure of the state. Indeed, today many protective measures are taken against cyber attacks, in light of the risks to national security and social order, the fear of systemic failure of essential services, the danger to the lives of citizens and macroeconomic scenarios. [For more information, visit the National Cyber ​​Directorate website.](https://www.gov.il/en/departments/israel_national_cyber_directorate/govil-landing-page)

**Hazardous Materials**

Emergency situations could emerge as a result of leaks of [hazardous materials (HMS)](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/articles/hazardous-materials/main/2803) - solids, liquids or gases, that may harm humans, life, plants, property or the environment.

A hazardous materials incident occurs when a hazardous material gets out of the control of the plant or any other entity that holds hazardous materials, and may occur routinely or in an emergency. There are a number of factors that may lead to the release of hazardous materials, including a technical malfunction in the production process, human error, poor storage, accidents during transportation, natural disasters, war damage, terrorist attacks, and more.

A leak of hazardous materials may endanger the population in the area and cause risks of poisoning, burns, fires, etc. Early preparation and implementation of behavioral guidelines during a hazardous materials incident can significantly reduce the risk and save lives. [Click here for life-saving guidelines for a hazardous materials incident.](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/articles/hazardous-materials/main/2803)

**Natural Disasters**

**Earthquake** - An earthquake is a common natural phenomenon that can cause serious damage to life and property. Wherever earthquakes have occurred in the past, earthquakes are expected to occur in the future. The location of the State of Israel on the Syrian-African rift and the fact that strong earthquakes have occurred in Israel in the past, [also place Israel under the threat of an earthquake,](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/articles/earthquake/preparation-coping/2501) which could occur at an unknown time and cause serious damage to life and property. Another earthquake is expected to occur in Israel, but it is not known when, and therefore it is important to prepare for it in advance. [For life-saving guidelines during an earthquake, click here.](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/life-saving-guidelines/earthquake/)

**Tsunami** - A tsunami is an extremely large surge of water. The word originates from the Japanese language and means - a wave hitting the sea ("tsu" - port, "nami" - wave). Throughout history, the coasts of Israel have been hit by deadly tsunami events. The tsunami scenario in Israel is for a wave 5 meters high, which will penetrate a kilometer inland and include several long waves, with the first wave not necessarily the highest wave. [Click here for life-saving guidelines during a tsunami.](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/life-saving-guidelines/fear-of-a-tsunami/)

**Fires** - The summer season in Israel is characterized by fires, which sometimes endanger large communities and areas. However, huge fires can also occur in the winter. For example, the large fire in Carmel at the end of 2010 highlighted the danger of a fire incident throughout the year. [Click here for guidelines on how to behave in the event of a fire.](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/articles/fires-storms/fires/2900/)

**Flooding** - Stormy weather in winter can cause flooding and landslides. This danger is especially prevalent in parking lots and elevators, as well as near streams and water sources.

**The importance of advance planning and preparation**

Most of us tend to minimize our estimation of the probability of a potential threat or the severity of its possible effects.  
When a crisis strikes, it’s natural to feel stress, confusion, and anxiety and to react instinctively, without rational thought.  
In these situations, there is no sense in looking for new answers.

Advance preparation for possible emergencies allows for a better transition from routine to emergency and improves functioning and response to unexpected incidents.

Also, in many cases, people are forced to deal with the emergency on their own, at least temporarily, until emergency services arrive. People have the basic ability to deal with these situations, but it depends a great deal on the skills and training they had before.

The time for learning, training, and preparing is during routine, through an organized and consistent process of preparation and instructions for practical steps. The goal is that when crisis strikes, we perform the necessary actions automatically, in a way that is influenced by part experience and previously gained information. Suitable preparation and practice giving a sense of success, will allow as smooth transition as possible from routine life to emergency actions, will contribute to a sense of security and control, and will reduce the level of surprise at time of emergency.

The following examples demonstrate the critical importance of advance preparation:

**Rocket and Missile Attack** - There are many examples from recent years in which individuals and families [who knew how to behave during a rocket fire alert took](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/life-saving-guidelines/rocket-and-missile-attacks/) immediate action and saved their lives.For example, in October of 2018, a woman from Be’er Sheva was in the protected room (Mamad) with her three children whilst their home sustained direct damage from a rocket fired from the Gaza. By staying there, her life and her children's lives were saved.  
In another case, in March 2019, a house in the moshav of Mishmeret was hit by a rocket fired from Gaza. Members of the family who managed to reach the protected room (Mamad) survived unscathed, whilst an additional member of the family who did not reach the protected room (Mamad) sustained moderate injuries.

**Earthquakes** - In case of an earthquake, there is a high risk of many deaths and injuries. However, appropriate conduct at the time of an earthquake is crucial in determining the outcome and the number of people trapped under the ruins.

The appropriate behavior includes moving to a safe place in the following order of priorities: exiting to an open area, entering a protected room (Mamad) or stairwell. If those options are unavailable, find cover under a heavy table or in a corner of the room.

### Steps for preparing for emergencies

Preparation for emergencies includes the following steps:

* **Knowing the guidelines for proper behavior when receiving an alert for the various threats -**  [guide to how to act during a siren](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/life-saving-guidelines/rocket-and-missile-attacks/), [for correct behavior during an earthquake](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/life-saving-guidelines/earthquake/), [for correct behavior during a hazardous material event.](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/life-saving-guidelines/hazardous-materials-event/)
* **Preparing your home -** select and prepare a protected space, clear escape routes and any other objects which may be a hazard. [Click here to read the guide on preparing your home for an emergency](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/articles/emergency-preparedness/preparing-routine/2102/).
* **Preparing your family** - when your first actions after an emergency are organized and divided between several people, your ability to cope improves and your chances of survival increase. [Click here to read the guide on preparing your family for an emergency.](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/articles/emergency-preparedness/preparing-routine/2102/)

Emergency Equipment and Communications

Emergency Preparedness

What is the right equipment to prepare for an emergency?

It is important to prepare in advance the necessary equipment that will assist us and our household members during an emergency, and to store it inside a bag that we can take with us when needed.  
For the emergency equipment list, [click here](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/articles/info/preparing-protected-space/1201/ "נערכים למצבי חירום עם כל הציוד הנדרש - איראן 7").

What emergencies require to prepare equipment ahead of time?

In emergency situations that require the population to remain in lockdown for an extended period of time, in situations where it is necessary to evacuate the house, and in situations in whichf damage to infrastructure may damage the fabric of life. A mass casualty incident also requires preparing equipment in advance because a large number of cases will force the rescue forces to set priorities for handling cases and will extend the time of arrival to each home and each patient.

What are the phone numbers of call centers that provide emotional support and help during emergencies?

[For a list of phone number for call centers that provide emotional support and help](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/articles/info/emergency-management/1307/ "חשים מתח? עצות להתמודדות").

Why is it important to prepare emergency phone numbers in advance?

Past experience shows that in emergency and distress situations, it is important to involve the emergency entities, the local municipality and other factors such as family members, acquaintances and neighbors, who can help as soon as possible. This is due to the following reasons:  

• Time in an emergency event is critical - in emergency and distress situations, it is important to involve relevant people and entities as soon as possible. A quick arrival of the emergency forces will help in a life-saving response. For example, a quick arrival of the fire and rescue forces will allow to rescue trapped people and put out the fire faster, and a quick arrival of medical forces will make it possible to provide first aid quickly, which can save lives.  
• Remote assistance - the emergency factors can provide essential guidance from a distance, for example in providing assistance to the injured until the arrival of the ambulance and paramedics. However, it is always better to be prepared to deal independently with possible scenarios.  
• The development of emergency events is unknown - during an emergency, there may be situations in which we will not be able to contact the emergency and rescue forces within a short time. That is why it is important to prepare in advance a phone list of family members, acquaintances and neighbors who can help in an emergency.

Why should water be prepared in emergency equipment?

There are emergency situations in which the regular water supply may be affected, so it is important to prepare water in the amount of three liters per person per day, for three days.
