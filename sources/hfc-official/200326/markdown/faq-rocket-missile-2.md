## Rocket and Missile Attack

How to act when receiving an alert for a rocket and missile attack?

Enter the optimal protected space within the available time to reach shelter, as detailed [here](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/life-saving-guidelines/rocket-and-missile-attacks/ "ירי רקטות וטילים - הנחיות מצילות חיים").

How should one act when entering a MAMAD, MAMAK, or shelter?

The door must be hermetically sealed by rotating the handle 90° upwards. Make sure that the external steel window and the internal glass window are closed until the end of the operation to prevent the entry of missile blast waves. If a double-pane glass window is installed, the panes must be removed in advance and placed outside the room.

How to act upon receiving a preliminary guideline for a rocket and missile attack?

Move in proximity to an optimal protected space where you can enter within the available time to reach shelter when you receive an alert.

Why did I receive an alert without a preliminary guideline?

A preliminary guideline is issued several minutes before an alert is received, giving you additional time to improve your position and reach the optimal protected space in your area.   
This guideline will be provided, whenever possible, only in areas where the shooting originates from distant locations.  
When the shooting originates from nearby areas, only an alert will be issued based on the time available to reach shelter.  
The alert will be received via a variety of means of alert: the siren system, the Home Front Command app, the National Emergency Portal, and supplementary platforms: Telegram, television, and radio  
Please note: If a preliminary guideline is issued and immediately followed by an alert, you must enter the protected space within your available time and not exit until you receive explicit instructions.

How long until we can leave the protected space after receiving the alert?

It is required to remain inside the protected space until a further notification is issued by the Home Front Command.

How would I know I can leave the protected space?

The Home Front Command will issue a notification to exit the protected space at the end of emergency events through the following channels:  

1. Home Front Command App.  
2. National Emergency Portal.  
3. Cell Broadcast (CB) System: A personal message sent with the device's standard notification sound.  
4. Home Front Command Telegram Channels.  
5. The following radio stations:  
Kol Barama: 105.7, 104.3, 92.1, 107.6 FM  
Kol Chai: 92.8, 93, 102.5 FM  
Radio Darom: 101.5 FM  
Galei Israel: 106.5, 94, 89.3 FM  
Weekends only - Kan Moreshet: 90.5 / 90.8 / 92.5 / 100.7 FM  
Additionally, you may call the Home Front Command emergency hotline at 104 and listen for updated instructions via the media.  
If you have no means of communication to receive the "all-clear" message (such as a landline, cell phone, internet, or radio), it is recommended to coordinate in advance with neighbors, family members, or the building committee to physically update you when it is safe to exit the protected space.

Why did I receive a preliminary guideline but no alert afterwards, and yet I still received an "end of event" notification to exit the protected space?

An "end of event" and exit notification is sent via the Home Front Command app and Cell Broadcast (CB) to the entire area that received the preliminary guideline—even if no alerts were triggered in that specific location—to officially conclude the event.

I don’t have the app / There is no reception in the protected space – how will I receive the instruction to exit?

The Home Front Command app is designed to function even with minimal reception. You can also bring an additional means of communication into the protected space, such as a battery-operated radio, to receive instructions and updates from local radio stations.

Will the IDF's defense systems protect us?

The IDF's defense systems do not provide a hermetic (100%) defense. Therefore, you must follow the Home Front Command guidelines and improve your preparedness during the upcoming period.

What should I do if a preliminary guideline or an alert is received while I am in an open area?

You should improve your position and reach an optimal protected space that you can enter once an alert is received.

If an alert is received, enter the optimal protected space within the time available to reach shelter. If this is not possible, lie flat on the ground and protect your head with your hands.

What should I do if a preliminary guideline or an alert is received while driving a vehicle?

* If a preliminary guideline is received, utilize the time to improve your position and move closer to an optimal protected space, which you can enter within the time available to reach shelter once an alert is received.
* If an alert is received and it is not possible to reach the optimal protected space within the time available to reach shelter, carefully pull over to the side of the road, exit the vehicle, move beyond the shoulder or the safety barrier, lie flat on the ground, and protect your head with your hands.

Should one take cover under bridges if an alert is received while driving?

One must not stop under a bridge upon receiving an alert. The bridge does not protect against a nearby impact, as it may be damaged by the blast and collapse. Furthermore, when a missile or interception debris hits the ground, the blast and shrapnel disperse diagonally from the sides; since the bridge is open on its sides, it provides no protection even when the impact occurs nearby.

What do you do when driving in a tunnel during an alert for rocket and missile attack?

If you are inside the tunnel and can safely stop in an emergency bay, you may stop there. If that is not possible, you must stop safely on the shoulder outside of the tunnel, exit the vehicle, and move away beyond the edge of the road or the guardrail, lie on the ground, and protect your head with your hands.

How should I act if a preliminary guideline is received while traveling on a bus?

If a preliminary guidelineis received and the bus is near an optimal protected space, stop safely and allow the driver and passengers to enter it.  
If an optimal protected space exists a short distance away and can be reached via a brief drive - proceed to it, stop, and allow passengers to exit safely.

How should I act if an alert is received while traveling on public transport?

**On an intercity road** - the driver must pull over and open the doors, and passengers must bend below the window line and protect their head with their hands.

**On a city road** - the driver must pull over to the side of the road and open the doors, and passengers must disembark and enter an optimal protected space within the defensive time.  
If an optimal protected space cannot be reached within the defensive time, crouch below the window line and protect the head with the hands.

**When traveling by train** - the driver must slow down to 30 km/h, and passengers must crouch below the window line in the carriages and protect their head with their hands.  
Upon reaching the nearest station, passengers must disembark carefully and enter the optimal protected space in the station.

**On the Light Rail** - on an above-ground line - the driver must stop in a safe place and open the doors, and passengers must crouch below the window line and protect their head with their hands.  
On an underground line - if the train is close to the tunnel exit, the train will stop and passengers must remain inside it.

What should you do if you receive an alert while you are in a prefabricated structure?

If you receive an alert while in a structure that does not include a concrete ceiling and 4 concrete or block walls, including a prefabricated structure, a portable structure, a trailer, and a plaster or wooden structure, you must exit the structure and enter the protected space during the time you have available to reach shelter. If you cannot enter the protected space in the time you have available to reach shelter, you must exit the structure, lie on the ground, and protect your head with your hands.

What should I do if a guideline is received to remain in proximity to a protected space?

When a guideline is received to remain in proximity to a protected space, you must stay nearby so that if an alert is received, you can enter it within the time available to reach shelter. If a preliminary guideline is issued, it will provide additional minutes to move closer and improve your position within the optimal protected space in your area.

What should I do if I find unexploded rockets, interceptor fragments, or submunition?

At this time, unexploded rockets and parts of missiles may be found on the ground. It is vital to remember that filming unidentified objects, staying near them, and—above all—any form of contact, are life-threatening actions!

If you are in an open area, you may encounter submunition or unexploded rockets that could explode upon contact and endanger your life.

Remember: If you see unexploded rockets or unidentified objects—Stay Away, Keep Others Away, and Report: Stay away from the site, keep bystanders at a distance, report immediately to the Israel Police by calling [100](tel:100).
