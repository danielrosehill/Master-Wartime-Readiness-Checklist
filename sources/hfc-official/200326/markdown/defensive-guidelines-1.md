## Managing Routine Life During War

What are the defensive guidelines?

The defensive guidelines include guidelines on managing the wartime routine of life in three areas: educational activity, workplaces and mass gatherings. The aim is to balance between saving lives and maintaining a routine and keeping the economy running. The guidelines are published for the public in the Defensive Policy document.

Is there a change in the Defensive Policy?

In accordance with the situational assessment of the Home Front Command, a change in the Defensive Policy has been decided upon, which will take effect starting Monday, March 16, 2026, at 6:00 AM: Some areas of the country will move to Level 2 – Partial Activity; the remaining areas of the country will stay at the Limited Activity Level. Until this policy takes effect, all areas of the country remain unchanged under the Limited Activity Level.

How is the Defensive Policy decided?

The Home Front Command conducts a daily situational assessment that examines the volume of fire, the population's ability to cope, available protection measures, the number of alerts, and intelligence assessments. It was decided to gradually restore activity where the threat is lower. Situational assessments are conducted at all times to maintain a balance between protecting citizens and maintaining routine as much as possible during an emergency.

What areas of life do the defensive guidelines apply to?

The defensive guidelines applies to the activity of workplaces, educational activity, mass gatherings and essential activities.

What do the defensive guidelines levels include?

The Home Front Command's defensive guidelines are divided into four levels that are marked with the colors green, yellow, orange and red. Each guidelines area is classified by color according to these levels, from green, where full activity without restrictions is allowed to red, in which only essential activity can be carried out.

Is there public transportation based on to the Level 4 defensive policy?

According to the Level 4 defensive policy - essential activity, public transportation operates in a limited format. Check with the relevant transportation company for travel hours.  
Please note, if you choose to leave the house, take into account that travel times may be longer than usual due to heavy traffic on the roads, so alerts are received - you will not be near an optimal protected space.  
Therefore, before making a decision, calculate the travel time, stay updated on traffic jams and heavy traffic and accordingly, make the best decision possible. If you estimate that you will not have time to reach your family members, you should stay where you are, and when receiving an alert or a preliminary guideline from the Home Front Command, enter the optimal protected space near you.

To find out whether an activity can be carried out, you must check the defensive policy to see what guidelines apply to the area where the activity will take place and what restrictions must be implemented in order to carry out the activity. Also, you must check with the institution, business or organization in charge of the activity, in what way it is conducted.

How long are the defensive guidelines valid for?

The guidelines within the defensive policy are published every day on all Home Front Command platforms and in the media and are valid to the date stated in them.

What are the guidelines areas?

Israel is divided into 30 guidelines areas that are used to communicate the daily defensive guidelines. The guidelines areas include towns with similar characteristics to allow keeping a routine of life when the home front is under attack. The names of the guidelines area are based on the names of known regions and geographical area, and every town in Israel belongs to one of the guidelines areas. For example, the city of Rishon LeZion belongs to HaShfela guidelines area and the city of Ashdod belongs to the Lachish guidelines are.

How can I know which guidelines apply to me?

To find out in time of emergency what are the guidelines in any area you are staying in – at home, at work, at your educational establishment, at an event any anywhere in the country – visit the National Emergency Portal or to the Home Front Command app and type the name of the town. A pop-up window will open showing the up-to-date guidelines. Alternitevely, you can all the Home Front Command hotline on [104](tel:104).

In view of the emergency declared in the country, what should I do?

It is imperative to plan the coming hours so that when an alert is received you will be able to enter the optimal protected space and have the necessary equipment for you and your family, based on the equipment that is in the house. In this context:  
1. The safest shelter for you should be determined, one that you can reach in the time available to reach shelter, according to the following options:  
• In the private domain - a protected room, a communal protected room or a communal shelter.  
• Public Protected Space – Standard protected spaces in educational institutions and municipal buildings, public shelters and public covers approved by the Home Front Command. Check with the local municipality where the public shelters are located and when they are open, before going to them.  
• If there are no public shelters in your vicinity, or if they are not opened, you may choose the stairwell or the interior space of your private domain.  
2. One should be prepared with essential equipment that is adapted to the family's characteristics and that will assist us in the protected space, such as water and food that does not require refrigeration, a charged phone and chargers, and essential medications.  
3. It is imperative that our family members be made aware of the situation and know what they should do.

What are the criteria for determining the Home Front Command's defensive guidelines

The criteria for determining the guidelines are based on daily situation assessments that are carried out at the Home Front Command and consider the danger to the population in a certain geographical area. This allows us to continue to function as a society, an economy and a state during the time of emergency.

Is it possible to enter an area that is under restrictions?

The Home Front Command's guidelines do not include permanent guidelines on travelling from one place to another. There may be certain roads that will be closed or areas that will be declared as closed military zones.

How wan I claim compensation for direct damage to property as a result of an act of war or a terrorist act?

In the event of direct damage to property as a result of an act of war or a terrorist act, contact the Tax Authority's emergency hotline on [\*4954](tel:*4954) or click on [this link](https://www.gov.il/he/service/online-direct-damage-claim).

Is public transportation available?

Public transportation is operating on a limited basis; you should stay updated on activity times with the Ministry of Transportation at \*4515.
