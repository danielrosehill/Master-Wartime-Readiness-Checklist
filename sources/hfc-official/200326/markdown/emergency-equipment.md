## In order to be able to cope optimally, it is important to prepare emergency equipment in advance and store it in the safe room. What equipment is important to prepare? Which equipment is essential for emergency and how to store it

Just as we plan for everything in our life, it is important that we prepare for emergencies. During an emergency, we may be asked to stay [secluded](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/articles/info/preparing-protected-space/1205) for a certain amount of time, or in some cases, evacuate from the house. Therefore, it is important to prepare the necessary equipment ahead of time, which will help us and our family members to deal with the emergency situation.

**Essential equipment for emergencies:**

* Water and food for the entire household members, for at least 3 days
* Emergency lighting
* Flashlight and batteries
* First aid kit
* Medicine and printed prescriptions
* Radio and batteries
* Portal charges for the mobile device
* Unique food according to the family characteristics (babies, elderly, animals, etc)

**Additional equipment recommended for an emergency:**

* Backup batteries for essential equipment such as medical devices
* Photocopy of essential documents such as ID card, driver's license, passport, **etc**.
* Fire extinguisher and smoke detector
* Cash to the extent that will satisfy the needs of the household members
* At least half a tank of fuel in the car
* Activities for children such as games, brochures and stationery
* Equipment required for animals

**Important emphases in preparing emergency equipment**

* It is recommended to store the equipment in a bag, which we can take with us when needed. It is important to first fulfill the need for essentials - water, food, medicine and means of communication.
* Items that are important to remain dry, such as documents and clothing, should be placed in sealed plastic bags.
* It is important to take into account the additional equipment that we will need if we are asked to stay in a protected space for a long amount of time, or to evacuate the house.
* It is important to involve the members of the household in compiling the list of emergency equipment, show them the contents of the bag and its storage location, make sure that everyone knows how to use the items in it.
* If there is a Protected Room (Mamad), the emergency equipment should already be stored in it. If there is equipment that needs to be refrigerated or stored elsewhere, it is important to indicate this to the members of the household and on the bag.
* It is important to carry out regular maintenance for the equipment:  
  Change the water bottles and check the food supply every three months.  
  Check the condition of the batteries and other electronic devices once every six months.  
  You can appoint a person responsible for performing periodic inspections and ongoing maintenance of the bag.
* You must verify the integrity of the gas containers and check their capacity - in private homes, it is important to make sure that the spare gas tank is full. In shared buildings, make sure that the storage tank is more than half full.

**Important points to remember when preparing emergency equipment for patients and people with disabilities**

* Medical documents that indicate your medical condition, including: regular medications, known allergic reactions, and communication difficulties.
* It is recommended to ask your family doctor for a medical status sheet, so that people know what to do if you need help, or what to avoid if necessary.
* It is recommended to ask your family doctor for a medical condition report, so that people know what to do if you need help, or what to avoid in case of need. If you require special food - prepare dry or canned food tailored to your unique nutritional needs.
* If you are dependent on a caregiver, wear a necklace or bracelet that identifies your condition as someone who needs assistance.
* For people who need medical equipment - it is important to make sure that there is a possibility of operating the medical equipment using batteries and that they have backup batteries and/or charging means.
* Contact list - a document with phone numbers and addresses of relatives, people, and organizations that can help.

**Points to remember for people with visual impairments**

* Specific equipment required, such as headphones and chargers - if the headphones are battery-operated, backup batteries and/or charging means should be provided.
* Emergency equipment should be marked in large handwriting, luminous tape, or Braille.
* A document with phone numbers and addresses in Braille or large print of relatives and people and organizations who regularly assist.
* **For those assisted by a service dog:**  
  Dog equipment: food, medication, a drinking and water bowl, a favorite toy, a leash, a mouth guard, and thick socks to put on the dog's feet, in order to prevent injury from broken glass and other sharp debris.
* Copies of the dog's license, certificates, vaccination certificates, and contact information for the veterinarian.
* For those assisted by a guide dog - Make sure the dog wears a collar with a tag indicating that it is a guide dog for the blind.

**Highlights for people with hearing disabilities**

* Specific equipment required for the hard of hearing and deaf: a hearing aid and spare batteries or an independent charger, cleaning kits and drying boxes. If the following devices are used routinely, an FM system, a personal amplifier, and a speakerphone that connects to the mobile phone must be prepared.
* Paper and writing utensils.
* List of sign language translators.
* A tag stating that you need communication mediation.
* If there is a dependence on sign language. Prepare a card identifying you as a deaf sign language speaker, and important messages such as: I only read lips.

**Emergency equipment for people with mobility disabilities**

* If you use a wheelchair - prepare a kit for puncture repair and pumping air in the wheelchair wheels or spare inner tubes and a pump. If necessary, equip yourself with a pair of heavy gloves for turning wheels on rough roads.
* If you use a motorized wheelchair or motorized scooter - prepare additional and available batteries, a charger and a non-motorized wheelchair.
* If you require electricity for survival - prepare backup batteries or a generator.
* Mobility aids (non-electric or motorized).
* For those with speech difficulties - a tag indicating that communication mediation is required
* Hygiene products
* Assistive tools - batteries for devices, chargers for batteries and devices, aids for mobility devices.

## Q&A

Emergency Equipment and Communications

What is the right equipment to prepare for an emergency?

It is important to prepare in advance the necessary equipment that will assist us and our household members during an emergency, and to store it inside a bag that we can take with us when needed.  
For the emergency equipment list, [click here](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/articles/info/preparing-protected-space/1201/ "נערכים למצבי חירום עם כל הציוד הנדרש - איראן 7").

What emergencies require to prepare equipment ahead of time?

In emergency situations that require the population to remain in lockdown for an extended period of time, in situations where it is necessary to evacuate the house, and in situations in whichf damage to infrastructure may damage the fabric of life. A mass casualty incident also requires preparing equipment in advance because a large number of cases will force the rescue forces to set priorities for handling cases and will extend the time of arrival to each home and each patient.

What are the phone numbers of call centers that provide emotional support and help during emergencies?

[For a list of phone number for call centers that provide emotional support and help](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/articles/info/emergency-management/1307/ "חשים מתח? עצות להתמודדות").

Why is it important to prepare emergency phone numbers in advance?

Past experience shows that in emergency and distress situations, it is important to involve the emergency entities, the local municipality and other factors such as family members, acquaintances and neighbors, who can help as soon as possible. This is due to the following reasons:  

• Time in an emergency event is critical - in emergency and distress situations, it is important to involve relevant people and entities as soon as possible. A quick arrival of the emergency forces will help in a life-saving response. For example, a quick arrival of the fire and rescue forces will allow to rescue trapped people and put out the fire faster, and a quick arrival of medical forces will make it possible to provide first aid quickly, which can save lives.  
• Remote assistance - the emergency factors can provide essential guidance from a distance, for example in providing assistance to the injured until the arrival of the ambulance and paramedics. However, it is always better to be prepared to deal independently with possible scenarios.  
• The development of emergency events is unknown - during an emergency, there may be situations in which we will not be able to contact the emergency and rescue forces within a short time. That is why it is important to prepare in advance a phone list of family members, acquaintances and neighbors who can help in an emergency.

Why should water be prepared in emergency equipment?

There are emergency situations in which the regular water supply may be affected, so it is important to prepare water in the amount of three liters per person per day, for three days.
