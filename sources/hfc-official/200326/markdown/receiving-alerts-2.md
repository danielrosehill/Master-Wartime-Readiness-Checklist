## Means of Alert

Why did I receive an alert without a preliminary guideline?

A preliminary guideline is issued several minutes before an alert is received to provide additional time to improve your position and reach the optimal protected space in your area. This guideline will be provided, whenever possible, only in areas where the shooting originates from distant locations.

When the shooting originates from nearby areas, only an alert will be issued based on the time available to reach shelter.

Please note: If a preliminary guideline is issued and is immediately followed by an alert, you must enter the protected space within your available time and not exit until explicit instructions are received.

How can I receive an alert according to my exact location?

The Home Front Command app enables you to receive an alert on your smartphone according to your exact location (make sure to enable location access on your phone). This way wherever you are, the app will recognise your location and send an alert if necessary.

Is there an alert in case of interception?

Yes, an alert will be activated when an interception is executed that endangers the population.

Why did I receive an alert saying "In your area" instead of my town's name? What should I do?

The Home Front Command app is a personal alert system that sends a notification based on a specific location. There are cases where residents living near open areas might receive a personal alert on the app even if a siren hasn't been activated for the entire town.  
Additionally, in areas where small towns are clustered closely together, the app may recognize the general vicinity as a single zone, resulting in an alert that says "In your area".  
In any case, if you receive an alert through any of the Home Front Command’s official channels, you must enter your protected space immediately.

How can I receive an alert in the National Emergency Portal?

The National Emergency Portal allows you to receive focused alerts on a variety of threats to your home. To receive real-time alerts, turn on the sound alerts by clicking the speaker icon next to the Alerts tab on the home page, and then define the name of the town you wish to receive alerts for, or an exact address if your city is divided into several alert zones, on the Alerts page. You can define up to 10 areas for which you will receive an alert (if you do not define areas, you will receive an alert for the whole country). **Please note: In order for the home alert to work, the National Emergency Portal needs to be open on your computer.**

How can I receive alerts on my TV?

When inside your home, you may receive alerts on TV; the alert will appear as subtitles on the right of your screen indicating the names of the authorities - for example, Ramla, Lod, etc. In a city which has been subdivided, the name of the zone which is subject to the alert will be displayed: for example, Be’er Sheva South. Television alerts are aired on channels: 11, 12, 13,14, 9 and 140.

In addition, you can download the Home Front Command App for TV, to be able to receive real-time alerts while watching all contents operating on an Android TV operating system. [Press here for more information](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/articles/info/iron-swords/1119/ "חדש! יישומון פיקוד העורף בטלוויזיה")

How do you get an alert by radio?

When at home or in the car, set your radio to the local station relevant to the area you are. Alerts on the radio will interrupt the broadcast and use the following wording: “Home Front Command alert in "\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_" (name of town.) In case the Homefront Command raises the alert regarding another threat. For example: in a tsunami, the alert which will interrupt the broadcast will include the type of incident and the required self-defense measures. For example: “Concern of a tsunami in "\_\_\_\_\_\_" (name of town), get away from the beach.

How can people who do not speak Hebrew receive alerts?

Via the Home Front Command app, depending on the language defined- Hebrew, Arabic, English or Russian.  
[Download the Home Front Command app for mobile to receive an alert on a variety of threats in the relevant language](https://onelink.to/8p5wpq).

How can Shabbat-observant citizens receive alerts on Shabbat?

**Radio Stations Operating as "Silent Stations"**  
The following radio stations cease their regular broadcasts on Shabbat and holidays. Through them, you can receive preliminary guidelines, alerts, and all-clear notifications to exit the protected space:

* Kol Barama: 105.7, 104.3, 92.1, 107.6 FM
* Kol Chai: 92.8, 93, 102.5 FM
* Radio Darom: 101.5 FM
* Galei Israel: 106.5, 94, 89.3 FM
* Kan Moreshet: 90.5, 90.8, 92.5, 100.7 FM  

  Please note: During Operation "Lion's Roar," Kol Chai and Kol Barama radio stations can also be received in the northern region.  
  Also, you can receive alerts only on "Radio 90" at 94.7 FM.

**Home Front Command App**  
If you have a smartphone, you can receive alerts via the Home Front Command app if there is a threat in your area (after enabling location-based services) and in 10 areas of interest. If you have an iPhone, make sure it isn't on silent mode. [Click here to download the App](https://onelink.to/8p5wpq)  

**The National Emergency Portal**  
You can connect to the National Emergency Portal (this website) before the holiday, click on the speaker icon next to the "Alerts" tab on the home page, set the area you will be staying at, on the "Alerts" page, and leave your computer on during the holiday. If there is a rocket and missile attack towards the area you defined, you will receive an alert through a sound and a display on the computer screen.

How can people who do not own a smartphone receive the Home Front Command alerts?

* It is recommended to open the windows in your home to help ensure you hear the sirens.
* On your home computer via audio and visual alerts on the National Emergency Portal. To do this, click the speaker icon next to the "[Alerts](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/alerts-history/ "Alert History")" tab on the homepage, then define the names of the towns for which you wish to receive alerts, and leave the computer screen open.
* Through the media – on TV channels, radio stations, and news websites.  
  Also, it is possible to receive the Home Front Command alerts through the radio stations
* Kol Barama: 105.7, 104.3, 92.1, 107.6 FM
* Kol Chai: 92.8, 93, 102.5 FM
* Radio Darom: 101.5 FM
* Galei Israel: 106.5, 94, 89.3 F
* Kan Moreshet: 90.5, 90.8, 92.5, 100.7 FM  

  Please note: During Operation "Lion's Roar," Kol Chai and Kol Barama radio stations can also be received in the northern region.  

  Also, you can receive alerts only on "Radio 90" at 94.7 FM.  

  Radio Kol Chai (92.8/93 fm), Radio Kol Barama (104.3/105.7 fm), will be operating during Shabbat in a “silent wave” format.

How do I set up alerts on the National Emergency Portal so that the computer and website stay active and do not disconnect?

Go to the National Emergency Portal.  
Activate the audible alerts (speaker icon).  
Click on "Alerts" (red button).  
Define the areas for which you wish to receive alerts and leave the website open on your computer screen.  
To prevent the computer screen from entering "Sleep Mode": Go to your computer's settings and ensure that the sleep mode is disabled.  
**In Google Chrome:** Click the Settings menu (three vertical dots in the upper corner), then click Performance (in the side menu). Ensure that "Memory Saver" is turned off. Additionally, add the National Emergency Portal URL under the section: "Always keep these sites active."  
**In Windows OS:** Open Settings via the Windows Start icon, click on "Power & Sleep," and set both "Screen" and "Sleep" options to "Never." It is also recommended to ensure the Screen Saver is disabled.  
For Laptops: It is highly recommended to keep the laptop connected to a power source throughout Shabbat to prevent the battery from running out.

Can a pager (beeper) be received or used to receive alerts?

Due to technological developments and new platforms that make alerts accessible to the entire population—including the deaf and hard-of-hearing community—it has been decided by the Ministry of Welfare and the Home Front Command that the use of pagers is no longer relevant. The best solution is the Home Front Command app.
