## Educational activities

To which educational activities do the Defensive Policy guidelines apply?

To which educational activities do the Defensive Policy guidelines apply?

**Formal Activity:** Guidelines for operating institutions that conduct studies, including daycares, kindergartens, and family nurseries (up to age 6), schools, after-school programs, special education, competitive sports activities within sports associations and unions (for children and youth up to age 18), higher education, and vocational training. These guidelines do not apply to boarding schools. Boarding school residential compounds are considered a place of residence.

**Informal educational activity** - including summer camps, youth movements, overnight summer camps, seminars, and extracurricular programs for children.

In our area there are restrictions on the activity of educational institutions, but in the area where my children study there are no restrictions, can they go to school?

Yes, the guidelines apply to the towns for which they were determined and there are no restrictions on travelling from one place to the other. If an alert is received while travelling, follow the guidelines for travelling in a vehicle.

Do boarding schools continue to operate in towns where educational activities are prohibited?

The boarding school activity depends on the type of boarding school.

**An open boarding school** - where the students arrive every morning and return home in the evening (without overnight accommodation) - will be closed.

**Boarding school with overnight accommodation** - educational activities will not take place, but the boarding school will continue to operate as a place of residence for the students staying there.

**Boarding school supervised by the Ministry of Education** - educational activities are canceled. Students who have families will return to their homes, while students who do not have families will remain at the boarding school along with the staff and view it as a place of residence in terms of the guidelines.

How to act when receiving a rocket and missile attack alert while the babies and toddlers are sleeping in daycare?

In general, it is required to act in accordance with the Home Front Command guidelines - when receiving a rocket and missile attack alert, enter the protected space. It is required to choose and prepare the protected space ahead of time: ⁠Mamad, Mamak, Mamam or Shelter. If there is no Mamad, Mamak, Mamam or shelter, we will choose a good space in the middle - an inner stairwell or an inner space inside the building.

**If the policy requires reaching a standard protected space** - you can only enter a Mamad, Mamak, Mamam or shelter.

**If there is a rocket and missile attack alert when there are no restrictions by virtue of the defensive policy:**

* If there is a Mamad, Mamak, Mamam or shelter - the toddlers must be brought into it, provided that this can be done within the time available to reach shelter.
* If there is no Mamad, Mamak, Mamam or shelter, or if it is not possible to enter one within the time available to reach shelter – it is required to act in accordance with the "most protected space" and make sure that the toddlers are lying on the floor in an inner space, which is surrounded by as many walls as possible and includes as few windows and openings as possible. They must lie next to an inner wall, below the window line and not in front of the door.

How should one act if a preliminary guideline or an alert is received during student transportation?

If a preliminary guideline is received during the journey and there are several minutes to reach a nearby standard protected space, the driver will prepare to stop in a safe location and the students must exit the bus and reach the standard protected space.  
If a standard protected space cannot be reached, the driver will carefully stop in a safe place at the side of the road and open the transportation vehicle's doors, and upon receiving the alert (siren), the students will remain in their places and bend below the window line.  
In the Central Command (Pakmaz) only - the driver will continue driving until reaching a safe place.

How will we know if the school is prepared in terms of protection for all students and staff?

In accordance with the guidelines of the Home Front Command and the Ministry of Education, schools are required to verify the integrity and readiness of the standard protected spaces, and educational staff have been briefed and have conducted drills. The responsibility to ensure that the protection solution is adequate and that the standard protected space can accommodate everyone present during the activity lies with the local municipality and the Ministry of Education.

How will I know if the school is opening?

You should stay updated via the educational institution or the local municipality regarding the status of educational frameworks in the municipality where you reside.

Why are children being returned to school?

The Home Front Command constantly conducts situational assessments, weighing the existing threats and the children’s safety against the great importance of returning the education system and the economy to activity. Returning to educational frameworks, stability, and routine is a significant part of building children's resilience. Studies will take place in specific areas and on a selective basis, in full coordination with the Ministry of Education and in accordance with the Home Front Command guidelines. The return to school is designed to enable a routine alongside the security reality, while strictly following guidelines and knowing how to act when an alert is received.

Why isn’t educational activity being restored in our area?

The Home Front Command conducts daily situational assessments and works as much as possible to enable an emergency routine for children and parents, recognizing the importance of routine for mental resilience and the daily functioning of citizens. However, the supreme value is the preservation of life; therefore, activity will be permitted **only in areas where it can be conducted safely and in accordance with the Defensive Policy guidelines.**

What happens if an alert is received during school hours?

If your children are currently at their educational institutions, it is important to remember that the educational staff by their side have been briefed and have conducted drills accordingly; they provide the children with all the support and the necessary response at this time.

What should be done if an alert is received on the way to or from school?

**When walking:** Check in advance and identify, together with your children, where standard protected spaces are located along the route to school and in the surrounding area. It is important to practice walking to and from school with them, including entering the protected spaces within the time available to reach shelter. Instruct the children that if preliminary guidelines are received, they should move toward the best possible protected space, and upon receiving an alert, enter it according to the time available to reach shelter.

**In public transport and school buses:** Upon receiving an alert, the driver will stop the bus at the side of the road and open the doors; passengers must crouch down, lower their heads below the window line, and cover their heads with their hands.

**Ending the incident and exiting the protected space:** Exiting the protected space will take place only after receiving an explicit instruction from the Home Front Command allowing you to leave.

Are private lessons permitted?

They may be conducted in accordance with the guidelines for workplaces.

Should I arrive immediately to collect the children during an alert, or a rocket or missile attack?

No. You must act in accordance with the Home Front Command guidelines and stay updated via the local municipality and the educational staff. Upon receiving an alert, do not leave the protected space until receiving an explicit instruction.

How to prepare our children before they return to their educational frameworks?

We should prepare our children and ensure they know what to do if an alert is received on the way to their frameworks or while staying in them. Explain to them that the role of the preliminary guidelines and the siren is to keep them safe, and that these notify them to reach the nearest protected space, just as we are used to doing together both at home and outdoors.

Ensure they are familiar with the time available to reach shelter in your residential area. Make sure the children understand that upon receiving preliminary guidelines on their phone, they should improve their location by moving toward a protected space; then, upon receiving the alert (the siren), they must enter the protected space within the time available to reach shelter and remain there until an explicit instruction is given by the Home Front Command. You can check the time available to reach shelter on the National Emergency Portal or the Home Front Command app. You can check the time available to reach shelter in your area in the National Emergency Portal or via the Home Front Command App.
