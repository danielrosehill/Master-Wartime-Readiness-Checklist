## Arrival Time to the Protected Space

How do I know what is the time available to me to reach a protected space?

Israel is divided today into 1,700 alert areas. Each area has a defined defense time, which is the available time to reach shelter. Most areas in Israel have a defense time of a minute and a half. Areas close to the borders have a designated defense time, which varies: immediate, 15 seconds, 30 seconds, 45 seconds, and a minute.  
To check the [defense time](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/articles/protection-guidelines/preparing-protected-space/1103/ "מה זמן הכניסה למרחב המוגן?") available to you, [click on this link](https://www.oref.org.il/eng/alerts-history/ "Alert History") and enter the name of your town (in case of a split city – your residential address).

How do I know the time available to reach shelter if I am in an area whose defense time is unknown to me?

In the event that we are in an area where the defense time is unknown to us, you should know that the length of the alert by Home Front Command sirents is the same as the time available to reach the protected space. That is, if an alert sounds in your area, you must reach the protected space before the alert ends. If you have not had time to enter a nearby building or shelter, lie on the ground and protect your head with your hands.

Does the time available to reach shelter in case of missile fire changes if the missile fire is coming from the north or from the south?

The time available to reach the shelter is set and does not change depending on the threat. The time that was set for each town is in accordance with the worse and most probable threat.   
The time available to reach shelter in most areas of the country was set to a minute and a half. In the areas closer to the border the time available to reach shelter that was set is either immediate, 15 seconds, 30 seconds, 45 seconds, a minute or a minute and a half.  
[For all the information on the time available to enter the protected space.](https://www.oref.org.il/heb/articles/protection-guidelines/preparing-protected-space/1103/ "מה זמן הכניסה למרחב המוגן?")

What to do if you cannot reach the protected space within the time available to reach shelter?

If you are unable to enter a nearby building or a shelter, lie on the ground and protect your head with your hands.

How long until we can leave the protected space?

it is required to stay in the protected space for 10 minutes or until you receive a message the event has ended.

May we leave the protected space after hearing an explosion?

No. Remain in the protected area for 10 minutes or until receiving notice that the event has ended.

What is the duration of the alert sounded by Home Front Command sirens?

The length of time in which the alarm sounds is the time available to reach shelter, ranging from immediate to 15 seconds, 30 seconds, 45 seconds, a minute and a minute and a half. If the alert ended and you have not yet reached the protected space, lie on the ground and protect your head.

Exceptions:

• In guidelines area North Golan Heights and in most towns belonging to the Confrontation Line guidelines area – the alert lasts for 15 seconds, but the time available to reach shelter is immediate. Therefore, when an alert is received, lie on the ground and protect your head with your hands.

• In the Gaza Envelope area – a Red Color alert will sound and the time available to reach shelter is 15 seconds.
